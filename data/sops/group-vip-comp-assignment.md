# VIP and Comp Room Assignment for Groups

**Property:** The Ironwood, Sedona, AZ
**Department:** Group Management
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Most group contracts at The Ironwood include complimentary rooms and VIP room provisions for the group's key decision-makers. Getting these assignments right — the right room for the right guest, set up at the right standard, properly coded — is one of the few touchpoints where the property's operational sophistication is directly visible to the people who decide whether to return with future group business. This procedure defines how comp and VIP rooms are identified, assigned, coded, and prepared for group arrivals.

## Scope

Applies to the General Manager (who handles group coordination), Front Desk Agents, Director of Housekeeping, F&B Supervisor, and Spa Manager. Covers comp rooms and VIP rooms under a group contract. Individual VIP identification outside groups is handled under the service standards VIP recognition SOP.

## Responsibilities

- **General Manager:** Identifies comp rooms and VIPs from the group contract and planning conversations; assigns specific rooms; coordinates amenity setup.
- **Front Desk Agent:** Executes the check-in for VIPs per the elevated standard defined here; escorts to room when appropriate.
- **Director of Housekeeping:** Performs elevated inspection of VIP rooms before arrival; coordinates amenity placement.
- **F&B Supervisor:** Prepares and delivers welcome amenities to VIP rooms; ensures any contracted meal or beverage is executed to standard.
- **Spa Manager:** Contacts VIP guests for any contracted spa services and arranges bookings in advance.

## Defining VIP and Comp in the Group Context

These terms have specific meanings in the group business context. Use them precisely.

### Comp Room

A room provided to the group at no charge per the contract. The comp rate is typically issued on a ratio basis — one comp per 40 paid room nights is a common industry convention but varies by contract. Comp rooms may or may not also be VIP rooms.

### VIP Room

A room designated for a group's key decision-maker — the meeting planner, the corporate executive, the wedding couple, the tour operator's representative. VIPs receive elevated service and amenities regardless of whether their room is comp'd or paid. Not every VIP room is a comp room; many VIPs pay the group rate for their own room.

### Key Distinction

Comp rooms are a billing construct (who pays). VIP rooms are a service construct (how the guest is treated). A room can be one, both, or neither. The group contract specifies both categories.

## Identifying VIPs from the Contract

At the block-setup stage (per Group Block Setup SOP), the GM reviews the contract and planning conversations to identify:

1. **Comp rooms** — the number specified in the contract. Confirmed with the group contact in writing before the group arrives.
2. **VIPs** — the specific names the group's contact identifies as requiring elevated service. Typical counts per group size:
   - 10–20 room block: 1 VIP (the meeting contact or couple)
   - 20–40 room block: 2–4 VIPs (contact, spouse, key stakeholder, CEO, etc.)
   - 40+ room block: 4–8 VIPs (broader leadership, speakers, sponsors)
3. **VIP tier** — some VIPs receive standard VIP treatment; others (the corporate CEO, the mother of the bride) receive elevated VIP treatment. The contract or planning call identifies tier.

The GM confirms VIP identification with the group contact 2 weeks before arrival in writing.

## Room Assignment

VIP and comp room assignments are made 7–10 days before arrival — earlier than standard room assignments to allow for preparation.

### VIP Room Assignment Criteria

1. **Best available room of the contracted type.** A VIP in a contracted king room gets the best king in inventory — highest floor, best view, newest soft goods.
2. **Proximity to group functions.** VIPs attending ongoing meetings should be near the meeting space; wedding VIPs should be near the ceremony and reception location.
3. **Noise considerations.** Not adjacent to elevator shafts, ice machines, or the party-floor of the block.
4. **View.** Red Rock views are reserved for VIPs when available.
5. **Accessibility.** Confirm any accessibility need is accommodated.

### Comp Room Assignment Criteria

1. Comp rooms may go to the same person as a VIP assignment (the group decision-maker gets both).
2. Comp rooms assigned to non-VIPs (e.g., the band's van driver, the meeting planner's assistant) are assigned to standard inventory, not the best rooms.
3. Billing coding must route the comp correctly per the Group Billing and Master Folio Management SOP.

### Common Mistakes

- Assigning a VIP to the worst room of the contracted type because it was the first one available in OPERA Cloud — VIP assignment is deliberate, not automatic.
- Giving a VIP a suite upgrade without Manager authorization — suite upgrades for VIPs are a pricing decision, not a service decision.
- Confusing a comp room with a VIP room and applying VIP amenities to a comp recipient who isn't a VIP — wastes amenities and is awkward if the guest doesn't know why they're getting the treatment.

## Billing Coding

Every comp and VIP room must be coded correctly in OPERA Cloud.

### Comp Rooms

1. Open the reservation for the comp room.
2. Change the room rate to zero.
3. Apply the group's comp rate code (typically configured at block setup).
4. Verify the folio instruction routes room and tax to the Group Master with a comp designation.
5. Confirm the reservation will not post against actual revenue at the audit — comp rooms count as occupancy but not revenue.

### VIP Rooms (Paid)

1. Standard group rate applies.
2. Mark the reservation as "VIP" in the OPERA Cloud VIP field.
3. Note the tier (Standard VIP or Elevated VIP) in the Reservation Notes.
4. Flag for the Manager or GM to personally greet at check-in.

### VIP Rooms (Comp'd)

Both coding steps above apply — the reservation is comp'd for billing and flagged for VIP service.

## Pre-Arrival Preparation

VIP and comp rooms receive elevated pre-arrival preparation.

### 48 Hours Before Arrival

1. GM confirms the final list of VIPs and comp room recipients.
2. Director of Housekeeping is notified of each room number requiring VIP preparation.
3. F&B Supervisor is notified of amenity requirements per guest.
4. Spa Manager reaches out to any VIP with a contracted spa service to schedule.

### 24 Hours Before Arrival

1. Director of Housekeeping personally inspects each VIP room for:
   - Soft goods condition (pillows, linens, towels)
   - Bathroom amenity presentation
   - Window and mirror clarity
   - Any surface dust or wear
2. Any issue identified is resolved before arrival day.

### Morning of Arrival

1. Room Attendant performs the VIP room clean to elevated standard, not standard departure clean. Extra attention to presentation details.
2. Director of Housekeeping performs a second inspection.
3. F&B Supervisor delivers welcome amenity to the room before the guest arrives:
   - Standard VIP: local Sedona specialty (prickly pear chocolates, local coffee), handwritten welcome note from GM, bottled water.
   - Elevated VIP: all of the above plus a bottle of wine or local spirit, additional handwritten note, fruit plate or cheese board sized appropriately.
4. Any contracted amenity (flowers, champagne, special dietary arrangements) is placed and timed to be at peak condition at the guest's arrival.

## At Arrival

### Check-In

1. When a VIP arrives at the desk, the FDA immediately notifies the GM or Manager on Duty by radio.
2. GM or Manager personally greets the VIP in the lobby, introduces themselves, and walks through the check-in alongside the FDA.
3. The check-in sequence is faster and more personal — less ID scrutiny (already confirmed in pre-arrival), more conversation about the trip and needs.
4. GM or Manager offers to escort the VIP to the room personally.

### Room Presentation

1. The GM or escorting Manager shows the guest the amenity personally.
2. Briefly highlights the view or a special feature of the room.
3. Provides personal contact info: "If anything comes up during your stay, here's my direct number."
4. Leaves the guest to settle in.

### Comp Room Arrival (Non-VIP)

Comp rooms where the recipient is not a VIP receive standard check-in — polite, efficient, no elevated attention. Over-treating a non-VIP can create awkwardness if the guest isn't aware why they're getting special attention.

## During the Stay

VIP guests receive ongoing attention without being smothered.

1. Housekeeping assigns the same Room Attendant to the VIP room for the duration of the stay where possible, to maintain a consistent experience.
2. F&B staff are briefed on VIP names at pre-shift; address VIPs by name when they visit F&B outlets.
3. A mid-stay check-in from the GM or Manager (not the FDA) is standard for stays of 3+ nights — a phone call or lobby hello, not an intrusion.
4. Any issue raised by a VIP flows directly to the GM. Not escalated to manager-on-duty first; direct.

## Post-Departure

1. The GM sends a personal thank-you email to each VIP within 48 hours of departure.
2. The email references something specific from the stay — a conversation, a meal, a moment. Generic thank-you emails don't build return business.
3. The VIP's Guest Profile is updated with notes from the stay for future reference.
4. Any feedback is captured in the Group Feedback Log for the group's contact and the post-departure reconciliation.

## Documentation

- **Group Master record in OPERA Cloud** — VIP and comp designations flagged at the reservation level.
- **VIP Preparation Checklist** — completed per VIP room; filed in the Group Folder.
- **Amenity Card** — specifies what was placed, signed by the F&B Supervisor.
- **VIP Profile Notes in the Guest Card** — updated post-stay for return-guest recognition.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the GM immediately if:
- A VIP is dissatisfied with any aspect of the room or service at arrival
- A contracted amenity is not available or delayed
- A comp room coding issue is identified that would result in a revenue posting
- A VIP requests something outside the standard amenity package
- A comp room recipient disputes being on the comp list (rare but does happen)
