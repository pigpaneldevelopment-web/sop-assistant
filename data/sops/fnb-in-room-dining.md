# In-Room Dining Order and Delivery Procedure

**Property:** Ridgeline Lodge, Steamboat Springs, CO
**Department:** Food & Beverage
**Document Owner:** F&B Supervisor
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

In-room dining at Ridgeline Lodge is one of the most common guest touchpoints, especially during ski season when guests arrive back at the lodge exhausted and want a quiet meal in their room. A smooth in-room dining experience — accurate order, appropriate temperature, delivered on time, billed correctly — drives outsized review impact. This procedure defines how orders are taken, communicated to the kitchen, delivered, and reconciled.

## Scope

Applies to the in-room dining order taker (after hours, the Night Auditor covers this role), Servers assigned to delivery, line cooks, and the F&B Supervisor. Covers breakfast, lunch, dinner, and late-night menus. Does not cover group or event catering.

## Responsibilities

- **Order Taker:** Accepts and enters the in-room dining order accurately; quotes a realistic delivery window; confirms charges.
- **Server (Delivery):** Prepares the tray per standard; delivers to the room; presents and closes gracefully.
- **F&B Supervisor:** Owns the service window; monitors orders on Silverware; resolves any exception.
- **Line Cook:** Prepares in-room dining orders with a priority flag; fires them to be plated for delivery timing.
- **Night Auditor:** Handles late-night in-room dining from 10:00 PM to kitchen close (11:00 PM daily).

## Service Hours and Menus

- **Breakfast menu:** 6:30 AM to 11:00 AM
- **Lunch menu:** 11:00 AM to 4:00 PM
- **Dinner menu:** 5:00 PM to 10:00 PM
- **Late-night menu:** 10:00 PM to 11:00 PM (limited selection)
- **Overnight:** 11:00 PM to 6:30 AM, no in-room dining. Guests are directed to the 24-hour pantry on the lobby level.

The in-room dining menu differs from the restaurant menu in two ways: (1) it excludes dishes that do not travel well (risotto, most seafood entrees, dishes that rely on a hot pass), and (2) it includes simpler items optimized for in-room delivery (chicken dishes, roasts, pasta in cream sauces, heavier soups).

## Taking the Order

1. Answer the phone within 3 rings: "In-room dining, this is [name], how can I help you?"
2. Confirm the guest's name and room number. Pull up the Guest Card in OPERA Cloud.
3. Review any Dietary Notes before taking the order. If an allergy is flagged, handle per the Allergy and Dietary Restriction Handling SOP. Confirm the allergy aloud before taking the order.
4. Take the order item by item. Read each item back exactly as the guest stated it.
5. Ask about beverages, starters, desserts once the main order is captured.
6. Quote the delivery window. Default windows: breakfast 25 minutes, lunch 30 minutes, dinner 45 minutes, late night 30 minutes. Add 15 minutes during peak (breakfast 7:30–9:30 AM; dinner 6:30–8:30 PM).
7. Read back the full order including price subtotals and the delivery fee.
8. Confirm the guest wants the total posted to the room folio.
9. Enter the order in Silverware, flagged "IRD — Room XXX." The ticket prints to the kitchen with an IRD priority stamp.
10. End the call warmly, confirming delivery time.

### Common Mistakes at Order Taking

- Rushing the read-back — every minute saved on the phone gets lost twice in kitchen corrections.
- Not checking for allergies because the guest seems to be in a hurry — the allergy check is non-negotiable.
- Quoting a best-case delivery time during peak service — guest expects 25 minutes, gets it in 50, and the service is already a failure on arrival.

## Kitchen Flow

1. Silverware routes the IRD ticket to the hot line printer with a red IRD stamp.
2. The line cook reviews the ticket, confirms items, and times the fire to match the stated delivery window.
3. The line cook plates for in-room service — tight, secure plating that holds on a covered tray.
4. At plate-up, the line cook calls "IRD up, room [XXX]." The F&B Supervisor or assigned Server responds immediately.
5. Plates are covered with IRD domes or covers for temperature retention during delivery.

## Tray Setup

Every in-room tray meets this standard before leaving the kitchen.

1. White linen tablecloth on the tray.
2. All plates covered.
3. Flatware rolled in linen napkin — one setup per guest.
4. Salt and pepper shakers (single-use packets for late-night).
5. Condiments specific to the order (ketchup for fries, butter for bread).
6. A small vase with a fresh flower in season; replaced daily.
7. Beverages: hot items in a thermal carafe for coffee or tea; cold items in glasses covered with a saran top.
8. Printed check with itemized order, total, and signature line.
9. Pen clipped to the check holder.

## Delivery

1. Server carries the tray on a covered rolling cart to the guest's floor via the service elevator.
2. Server knocks on the room door firmly three times and announces "In-room dining."
3. Wait 10 seconds. Knock and announce again if no response.
4. When the guest opens the door, greet them by name if possible: "Good evening, Mr. Anderson — here's your dinner."
5. Ask where the guest would like the tray. Many guests prefer the desk; some prefer the dining table if the room has one.
6. Set the table tableside: remove covers with a small "tableside reveal," arrange flatware, pour any beverage that needs pouring, and describe each plate briefly.
7. Present the check. "Whenever you're ready, this will go to your room — just a signature for your records."
8. Confirm the guest doesn't need anything else: additional condiments, bread, water.
9. Tell the guest how to return the tray when finished: a phone call, a tray hanger in the hallway, or in the elevator bay per property standard. At Ridgeline Lodge, the standard is "call the desk and we'll come get it."
10. Thank the guest and leave the room promptly. Do not linger.

## Tray Retrieval

1. Guests call the desk when their meal is complete.
2. The Night Auditor or Server on duty retrieves the tray within 15 minutes.
3. All items return to the kitchen for wash; china does not sit in hallways.
4. Any left-over food, flatware, or glassware is counted against the delivery manifest. Missing items are flagged in the IRD Log.

## Late-Night Service (10:00 PM to 11:00 PM)

1. Late-night orders are taken by the order taker (or Night Auditor after desk-side order-taker off at 10:30 PM).
2. Late-night menu is limited: soups, salads, charcuterie, burgers, pasta, pizza, dessert, beverages.
3. Kitchen closes at 11:00 PM. Orders received at 10:55 PM or later are regretfully declined. "The kitchen has just closed — may I offer you a selection from our 24-hour pantry instead?"
4. After 11:00 PM, the 24-hour pantry on the lobby level is available. The Night Auditor can describe options or walk a guest down.

## Billing

- All in-room dining is posted directly to the room folio via Silverware-to-OPERA Cloud integration.
- The delivery fee is a separate line on the check (currently $8).
- Gratuity is added automatically at 18%, itemized on the check.
- Guests may add additional gratuity on the signature line; that amount is added to the folio.
- Any dispute at check-out is handled per the Folio Dispute Resolution section of the Departure and Check-Out SOP.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the F&B Supervisor (or Night Auditor after 11:00 PM, to relay to MOD) if:
- Delivery runs more than 15 minutes past the quoted window
- A guest is dissatisfied with the food and wants a remake or credit
- An allergy incident occurs (see the Allergy and Dietary Restriction Handling SOP and the Medical Emergency Response SOP)
- The kitchen cannot prepare an ordered item (86'd mid-service)

## Documentation

- **IRD Log in Silverware:** Every order logged automatically with timestamps.
- **Exception notes:** Added to the ticket in Silverware for any delay, remake, or credit.
- **Tray return tracking:** The Server records tray return time in the IRD Log.
