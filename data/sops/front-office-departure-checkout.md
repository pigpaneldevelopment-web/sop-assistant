# Departure and Check-Out Procedure

**Property:** Salinas Point Inn, Central California Coast
**Department:** Front Office
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Check-out is the last impression the property makes on a guest — and the last chance to ensure billing is accurate, the room is released to housekeeping promptly, and the guest leaves feeling known. A sloppy check-out erodes everything the stay built. This procedure defines the standard check-out sequence at Salinas Point Inn and how departure flows into housekeeping turnover.

## Scope

Applies to all Front Desk Agents and Night Auditors handling check-out before 7:00 AM. Covers standard check-out, express check-out, late check-out, and group departures. Group folio reconciliation is covered under the Group Billing and Master Folio Management SOP.

## Responsibilities

- **Front Desk Agent:** Performs the check-out sequence; reconciles the folio; releases the room to Housekeeping in Mews.
- **Night Auditor:** Handles early departures before 7:00 AM using the same sequence.
- **Director of Housekeeping:** Receives real-time room status updates from Mews as rooms are checked out.
- **Manager / General Manager:** Approves any disputed charge or comp exceeding $50.

## Timing

- Standard check-out: 11:00 AM
- Express check-out: available any time after midnight via the Mews guest portal
- Late check-out: granted by the FDA up to 1:00 PM at no charge when occupancy allows; 1:00 PM to 3:00 PM at 50% rate (Manager approval); past 3:00 PM is a full night's charge (Manager approval)

## Pre-Shift Preparation

At shift start, the Front Desk Agent prepares for the day's departures:

1. Open the Departures tab in Mews, filtered to today.
2. Review each folio for any anomalies — large incidental charges, missing postings, pending credits.
3. Identify any VIP or return guest departures and prepare a handwritten note or small parting amenity if appropriate.
4. Identify any group departures and coordinate with the group contact on master folio review timing.
5. Spot-check for any guest still showing "In-House" from prior days — these are possible stayover-to-departure transitions that need verification.

## Standard Check-Out Sequence

1. Greet the guest by name within 10 seconds: "Good morning, Mr. Patel — heading out today?"
2. Pull up the folio in Mews by last name or room number.
3. Confirm departure: "Checking you out of room 214 — does that sound right?"
4. Ask about the stay. Listen to the answer. This is the single highest-signal data point the property gets about its operation, and it must not be skipped.
5. Review the folio with the guest. Call out the final balance and any incidentals — minibar, F&B, parking — by name. Do not summarize vaguely.
6. Ask which email address they'd like the final folio sent to. Confirm the address on the Guest Card is current.
7. Process the payment method on file. If the guest wants to use a different card, process and update the Guest Card.
8. Email the final folio via Mews. Confirm with the guest that it has arrived.
9. If the guest mentions a service issue during the stay-review conversation, handle per the Complaint and Service Recovery SOP. Do not defer a legitimate complaint to a post-stay email — resolve it in person when possible.
10. Offer to arrange transportation, luggage assistance, or any final request.
11. Close with a return-invitation: "We'd love to have you back — we hope the drive home is easy."
12. In Mews, process the check-out. This automatically updates the room status to "Vacant — Dirty" and notifies Housekeeping via HotSOS.

## Express Check-Out

Guests who opted into express check-out via the Mews guest portal do not need to stop at the front desk.

1. The Night Auditor processes express check-outs as part of the overnight routine, marking rooms for departure in Mews.
2. Final folios are emailed automatically at 7:00 AM.
3. If a guest does stop at the desk after express check-out, confirm the departure and handle any question on the folio.

### Common Mistakes

- FDA skips the stay-review question because the guest "seems in a hurry" — the one-line version is always welcome: "How was everything?"
- Final folio emailed to an outdated address on file because the FDA didn't confirm at check-out.
- Room marked checked out in Mews but key cards not collected or returned — keys walk off at a higher rate than most properties realize.

## Late Check-Out Request

When a guest asks for a late departure:

1. Check the Arrivals Report for the room's turn time. If the room is not needed before 1:00 PM, grant up to 1:00 PM at no charge.
2. For 1:00 PM to 3:00 PM, consult the Arrivals Report carefully. If the room is needed for an early arrival of the same type, decline politely and offer luggage storage and lobby access instead.
3. For any late check-out past 3:00 PM, contact the Manager. Anything granted past 3:00 PM is a full night's charge unless the Manager waives it.
4. Update the Departure Notes in the Guest Card with the approved departure time so Housekeeping does not attempt to clean early.
5. Notify the Director of Housekeeping directly for any late check-out granted past 1:00 PM so the daily cleaning plan can adjust.

## Folio Dispute Resolution

If the guest questions a charge:

1. Pull up the posting in Mews. Show the guest the backup: the POS ticket for F&B, the timestamp for parking, the signed receipt if available.
2. If the charge is clearly correct and backup is clean, explain politely and hold the position.
3. If the guest remains dissatisfied and the disputed amount is under $50, the FDA may remove the charge without Manager approval — document the reason in the folio notes.
4. Above $50, call the Manager before removing any charge.
5. Do not argue. The goal is resolution, not vindication.

## Housekeeping Release

The moment a check-out is processed in Mews:

1. The room status updates to "Vacant — Dirty" automatically.
2. HotSOS notifies the assigned Room Attendant via tablet.
3. The Director of Housekeeping's dispatch board reflects the new departure in real time.

The Front Desk Agent does not separately contact Housekeeping unless:
- The guest reports damage or an unusual condition ("there was a spill on the carpet," "the shower drain is clogged")
- The guest left behind a found item that the FDA can see
- The guest was granted a late departure that changes the cleaning sequence

In those cases, send a Mews message to the Director of Housekeeping with specifics.

## Post-Departure

1. Keys returned are wiped and replaced in the key drawer for re-programming.
2. Any review feedback from the stay-review conversation is logged in the Guest Card for future reference. Positive notes and negative notes both belong here.
3. If a service recovery credit was applied, log the incident per the Complaint and Service Recovery SOP.
4. The review solicitation email is sent automatically by Mews 24 hours after check-out — no manual action required.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the Manager immediately if:
- The guest disputes a charge over $50
- The guest reports an issue during check-out that was never logged during the stay
- A group departure has a materially different folio balance than the contract projected
- Key cards cannot be accounted for and the room has been released
