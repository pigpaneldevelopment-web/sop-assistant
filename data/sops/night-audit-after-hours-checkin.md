# After-Hours Check-In Procedure

**Property:** The Pelican House, Destin, FL
**Department:** Night Audit
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

After-hours check-in at The Pelican House is a solo operation. A single Night Auditor is on the desk from 11:00 PM to 7:00 AM and may handle check-in while also monitoring the overnight audit, answering phones, and serving other in-house guests. This procedure defines how after-hours check-ins are handled safely, efficiently, and to the same guest experience standard as daytime check-ins — while accounting for the heightened security considerations of overnight.

## Scope

Applies to the Night Auditor. Covers check-in between 11:00 PM and 7:00 AM for expected late arrivals, walk-ins, and any arrival after the evening FDA has left the desk. Excludes group arrivals, which are coordinated in advance per the Group Block Setup SOP.

## Responsibilities

- **Night Auditor:** Executes the after-hours check-in per this procedure; maintains front desk security; escalates to the Manager on Duty if any situation falls outside standard handling.
- **Manager on Duty:** Available by cell phone for any after-hours escalation; does not come on property unless required by the situation.
- **General Manager:** Reviews after-hours check-in log and any exception in the morning operations check-in.

## Security Considerations Specific to Overnight

After-hours is not after-service — the standard of care is identical. But the security context is different.

- **The lobby is typically unlocked.** Anyone can walk in. Know who is in the lobby at all times.
- **No second staff member is on property (weeknights).** Backup is by phone.
- **Guest identity verification is tighter.** Daytime comfort cues (the guest was expected, the guest has a voucher, the guest is with a group) may not apply overnight.
- **Ambient noise matters.** Conversations carry across a quiet lobby into guest rooms.

The Night Auditor does not compromise on security under time pressure, but also does not make a legitimate guest feel interrogated.

## Pre-Arrival Awareness

At the start of every overnight shift, the Night Auditor prepares for expected late arrivals.

1. Review the Arrivals Report in Mews, filtered to "Pending Arrival."
2. Identify any arrival the evening FDA flagged as a late-arrival communication (Guest Card notes).
3. Have a Late Arrival List at the desk — printed or on-screen — with names, confirmation numbers, and any special notes.
4. Pre-program keys for expected arrivals if and only if the FDA has confirmed rate, ID, and payment. If any detail is pending, keys are programmed at arrival.

## Standard After-Hours Check-In Sequence

The sequence mirrors the daytime check-in with overnight-specific modifications.

### Greeting

1. Greet the guest promptly. Rise from seated position if seated.
2. Keep voice at a conversational indoor level. Avoid cheery or projective volume that carries into the lobby at 1 AM.
3. "Welcome to The Pelican House — checking in tonight?"

### Identity and Reservation Verification

1. Request photo ID before any reservation detail is discussed. This is the one meaningful overnight modification: do not confirm a reservation by name or room before ID is on the desk.
2. Take the ID and cross-reference with the Arrivals Report on-screen. The name on the ID should match a pending arrival.
3. If the guest's name does not match a pending arrival, do not proceed to room assignment. Politely ask for the reservation confirmation number and recheck.
4. If still no match, the guest may be a walk-in — see the Walk-In Reservation Handling SOP for overnight walk-ins.

### Reservation Confirmation

1. Confirm reservation details aloud at low volume: "I have you booked for two nights in the Gulf View King, checking out Thursday — does that sound right?"
2. Do not read rate aloud if a third party is in the lobby within earshot.
3. Ask about any special request before processing payment: "Anything else we can set up for you tonight?"

### Payment Authorization

1. Process the standard incidental hold per the Arrival and Check-In Procedure SOP: $75 per night plus room and tax.
2. If the hold fails, pause and discreetly explain: "Let me try that once more." Do not announce the failure.
3. If a second attempt fails, step away from the desk silently to call the Manager on Duty. Do not confront the guest about payment in the lobby.

### Key Programming and Orientation

1. Program the keys at the desk if they are not already pre-programmed.
2. Hand the key packet over with the Wi-Fi password, property map, and note about overnight security: "The lobby doors are unlocked — if you need anything overnight, I'm here until 7."
3. Provide a brief orientation — ice machine location, elevator, any amenity currently open.
4. Do not include spoken orientations to amenities that are closed for the night (restaurant, bar, pool). Provide these via a printed amenity card instead.

### Escorting

1. For a single late-arriving guest with heavy luggage, offer to escort to the room. Lock the desk drawer and post a "Back in 10 minutes" sign with a phone number.
2. Do not escort at 2:00–4:00 AM window when the audit is running — if the guest needs assistance during that window, walk the room only after audit completion.

## Verification Flags

The following trigger additional verification before proceeding.

### ID Cannot Be Verified

1. The ID is expired: request a secondary ID or a credit card with matching name.
2. The ID appears tampered with or is blurred beyond reasonable wear: politely decline and call the Manager on Duty.
3. The ID is from a country the Night Auditor doesn't recognize: confirm name matches the reservation, confirm the photo matches the guest, and proceed if both check out. Do not refuse service based on country of origin.

### Guest Appears Impaired

1. If the guest appears intoxicated but is calm and cooperative: complete check-in at a slower pace; ensure the guest can reach the room safely.
2. If the guest appears impaired to a degree that poses safety risk: contact the Manager on Duty before proceeding. See the Intoxicated Guest — Refusal of Service SOP.

### Guest Is Accompanied by a Third Party Who Remains Outside

1. Complete check-in normally if the guest is behaving normally.
2. Note the situation in the Shift Notes.
3. If anything feels off — guest is evasive, the third party is giving instructions through the door, the guest appears to be under duress — pause check-in and call the Manager on Duty. See also the Human Trafficking Indicator Recognition SOP.

### Rate or Room Type Discrepancy

1. If the guest expected a different rate or room type than what Mews shows: do not adjust the reservation without Manager approval.
2. Explain politely: "I see a difference between what you're expecting and what's booked here. Let me note this and the Manager can get back to you first thing in the morning. I'll get you checked into what we have booked for tonight."
3. Document the discrepancy in the Guest Card.

## Late Walk-Ins

Walk-ins after 11:00 PM are handled per the Walk-In Reservation Handling SOP with overnight modifications:

1. ID verification is required before any rate quote.
2. Cash walk-ins require photograph of ID attached to the cash receipt.
3. Any hesitation about the booking — impaired guest, evasive behavior, questions about other guests — triggers escalation to the Manager on Duty before completing.

## Common Failure Modes

- Skipping the ID-first rule because the guest is clearly tired and the Night Auditor wants to move quickly — the rule exists for cases that don't announce themselves.
- Escorting a guest away from the desk during the 2–4 AM audit window — the audit needs active monitoring.
- Explaining a rate dispute at the desk overnight — these are Manager decisions, and the Night Auditor shouldn't feel obligated to resolve them on the spot.
- Checking in a guest whose name does not match the reservation by assuming they're on the same reservation — verify the name on the ID matches either the reservation or is listed as an additional guest on the reservation before proceeding.

## Documentation

- **Mews Guest Card** — all standard check-in data captured.
- **Shift Notes** — any exception, escalation, or unusual situation logged.
- **Late Arrival Log** — paper log at the desk noting time of arrival for every after-hours check-in.
- **Incident Log** — any security flag or escalation documented.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the Manager on Duty by cell phone if:
- Guest ID cannot be verified
- Payment authorization fails on two methods
- Guest appears impaired beyond cooperative
- Guest is accompanied by third party in a way that raises concern
- Room assigned is not ready or is in unexpected status
- Any complaint is raised at check-in that cannot be resolved by the Night Auditor
