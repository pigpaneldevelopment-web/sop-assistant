# Group Block Setup and Rooming List Processing

**Property:** Juniper Creek Ranch, Fredericksburg, TX
**Department:** Group Management
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

This procedure governs how a group block is built, how a rooming list is processed into reservations, and how the property team is aligned on group details before arrival. Juniper Creek Ranch's group business is dominated by weddings and small corporate retreats — blocks typically run 20 to 50 rooms over 2 to 4 nights. A clean setup in Maestro upstream prevents almost every group arrival problem downstream.

## Scope

Applies to the General Manager, Revenue Manager (who also handles group coordination at this property), Front Desk Agents, Director of Housekeeping, and F&B Supervisor. Covers the period from signed contract through 48 hours before group arrival.

## Responsibilities

- **General Manager / Revenue Manager:** Owns the contract, builds the block in Maestro, manages the client relationship, and signs off on rooming list finalization.
- **Front Desk Agent:** Processes the rooming list into individual reservations; flags any data issue to the GM.
- **Director of Housekeeping:** Receives the final room assignment list 48 hours before arrival; plans labor and supplies to match.
- **F&B Supervisor:** Receives the group details 72 hours before arrival; coordinates any contracted meals or welcome receptions.

## Timeline

Every group block follows the same timeline against the arrival date.

| Milestone | Timing | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Block built in Maestro | Within 48 hours of signed contract | GM / RM |
| Cutoff date set and confirmed with client | At block build | GM / RM |
| Rooming list requested from client | 30 days before arrival | GM / RM |
| Rooming list received and processed | 21 days before arrival | GM / RM → FDA |
| Room assignments finalized | 7 days before arrival | GM / RM |
| Group recap distributed to team | 72 hours before arrival | GM / RM |
| Pre-arrival prep complete | 48 hours before arrival | All |

## Step 1 — Building the Block in Maestro

Within 48 hours of contract signature, the Revenue Manager builds the block.

1. Open the Group Module in Maestro. Create a new Group Master record.
2. Enter group name, contact name, contact phone and email, contract date.
3. Enter the group rate, rate code, and whether the rate is commissionable.
4. Enter the block by date and room type. For a typical wedding block, enter the peak night first and ladder the shoulder nights out.
5. Enter the cutoff date — the date by which the client must finalize the rooming list and after which unused rooms release back to general inventory.
6. Enter billing instructions per the contract: master folio items, guest room and tax responsibility, incidentals.
7. Enter any contracted concessions (comp rooms, welcome amenity, upgrade program, F&B minimums).
8. Save the Group Master and confirm the block count is visible on the Group Pickup report.

### Common Mistakes at Block Build

- Building the block against the wrong rate code and having to rebuild when the first reservation posts incorrectly.
- Forgetting to extend the block against shoulder nights when the contract clearly includes them.
- Not flagging the cutoff date on the GM's calendar, which then slips past without a client follow-up.

## Step 2 — Rooming List Processing

The rooming list is requested 30 days out and due 21 days out. Clients miss this date frequently. A standing follow-up email at 28 days out prevents most slippage.

### Reviewing the Received Rooming List

1. Open the rooming list as submitted by the client (typically an Excel or CSV).
2. Confirm each row has: guest name (first and last), arrival date, departure date, room type, any special request, any credit card for incidentals if the guest is responsible for room charges.
3. If any row is missing data, reply to the client with the full list of gaps in a single email. Do not send multiple small requests.
4. Verify total rooms requested matches the block. If the group is tracking below the block, flag for attrition discussion per the Attrition Clause Tracking SOP.
5. Confirm any VIPs on the list. Flag to the GM for pre-arrival amenity planning.

### Entering Reservations in Maestro

1. From the Group Master screen, use the "Add Guest to Block" function. Do not create reservations outside the block — orphaned reservations do not count toward pickup and cause billing errors.
2. Enter each guest one at a time. Copy guest name, dates, and rate from the rooming list. Verify each reservation attaches to the correct Group Master.
3. Capture the room type preference exactly. If the client requested a specific bedding configuration, note it in the Reservation Notes.
4. For guests responsible for their own incidentals, enter the payment instructions accurately — some couples pay for the full block, some only for room and tax, some only for a designated suite.
5. Record any special request (early arrival, late departure, special occasion, ADA accommodation) in the Reservation Notes. Flag ADA requests for Director of Housekeeping immediately.

## Step 3 — Room Assignment Finalization

Seven days before arrival, the Revenue Manager finalizes room assignments.

1. Open the Rooms Forecast in Maestro. Confirm the group does not conflict with transient demand or other groups.
2. Block rooms by assigning specific room numbers to specific reservations. Consider: proximity for wedding parties wanting adjacent rooms, accessibility for ADA guests, view for honeymoon suites.
3. For wedding blocks, block the bridal suite and parents' rooms on the same floor if possible.
4. Do not pre-assign rooms more than 7 days out — it blocks flexibility for incoming transient demand.
5. Send the Director of Housekeeping the final room assignment list via email for labor planning.

## Step 4 — Group Recap Distribution (72 Hours Before Arrival)

The Revenue Manager distributes the Group Recap to the team 72 hours before arrival. The recap includes:

- Group name, contact name and phone
- Arrival date, departure date, and total nights
- Total room count, rate, and billing setup
- List of VIPs and their room assignments
- List of ADA or special-request rooms
- Contracted F&B events with times and locations
- Any welcome amenity plan
- Contact for any on-site issues during the stay

Recap is emailed to: General Manager, Front Desk Agents on shift during the arrival window, Director of Housekeeping, F&B Supervisor, Night Auditor covering the arrival night.

## Step 5 — Pre-Arrival Prep (48 Hours Before)

1. Director of Housekeeping confirms labor and supply plan for the group's peak night.
2. F&B Supervisor confirms contracted meals are on the BEO and that the kitchen is prepped.
3. Front Desk Agent confirms all group reservations in Maestro show the correct rate, correct dates, and correct billing instructions. Spot-check five at random.
4. Welcome amenities (if contracted) are prepared and staged for placement on the morning of arrival.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the General Manager immediately if:
- The rooming list is materially different from the contract (room type counts, dates)
- A guest on the list is not in Maestro 48 hours before arrival
- An ADA accommodation cannot be satisfied with available inventory
- The group is trending below attrition threshold (see Attrition Clause Tracking SOP)
