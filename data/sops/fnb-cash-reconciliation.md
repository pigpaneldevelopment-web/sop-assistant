# End-of-Shift Cash Reconciliation (F&B)

**Property:** The Cannon & Vine, Nashville, TN
**Department:** Food & Beverage
**Document Owner:** F&B Supervisor
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

End-of-shift cash reconciliation at The Cannon & Vine protects the property, the team, and the guest. A clean reconciliation catches ticket errors while the shift memory is fresh, confirms the right revenue hits the right accounts, and closes out every server drawer and bar cash drawer with variance documented. This procedure defines the closing sequence for servers, bartenders, and the F&B Supervisor, and how discrepancies are investigated.

## Scope

Applies to all Servers, Bartenders, and the F&B Supervisor on closing shift. Covers the restaurant and lobby bar. Does not cover banquet event reconciliation — banquet close is governed by a separate SOP.

## Responsibilities

- **Server / Bartender:** Completes their individual close-out per the sequence below; reconciles their bank; submits cash and credit reports to the F&B Supervisor.
- **F&B Supervisor:** Verifies each server's close; reconciles the shift totals; deposits cash to the safe; resolves any variance; closes the shift in Toast.
- **General Manager:** Reviews the F&B Daily Revenue Report each morning; investigates any variance exceeding $25 from the previous night.
- **Night Auditor:** Receives the F&B close confirmation and matches F&B revenue to POS in the overnight audit per the Automated Night Audit Monitoring SOP.

## Closing Sequence Overview

End-of-shift close runs from last call (typically 10:30 PM on weeknights, 11:30 PM on weekends) through the F&B Supervisor's final deposit to the safe. Target completion: 60 minutes after last guest departure.

## Step 1 — Final Table Close-Out (Servers)

Before any reconciliation begins, every open table must be closed.

1. Server walks the final check to each remaining table, processes payment, and fully closes the ticket in Toast.
2. No ticket is left in "Open" status overnight. Any ticket that cannot be closed (guest walked, guest disputed, card declined) is flagged to the F&B Supervisor for handling before the server clocks out.
3. Any ticket that cannot be resolved at the table results in a "Shift Incident" entry in Toast. The Supervisor handles the next step.

## Step 2 — Server Close-Out Reports (Servers)

Each server prints their close-out report from the Toast server terminal.

1. The close-out report shows: total sales, total cash, total credit, total tips, comps, voids, and discount totals.
2. Print three copies: one for the server, one for the Supervisor, one attached to the cash deposit bag.
3. Review the report for anomalies — unusual void count, large comp totals, any discrepancy the server knows about.

## Step 3 — Cash Drawer Count (Servers and Bartenders)

Each server and bartender counts down their cash.

1. Pull the shift bank (typically $200 for servers, $400 for bartenders per the Restaurant Opening SOP).
2. Count the cash collected during the shift.
3. Subtract the opening bank to isolate shift cash.
4. Compare shift cash to the Toast close-out report's cash total.
5. If the count matches, place the shift cash in a pre-printed deposit bag labeled with server name, date, and amount.
6. If the count does not match, flag to the F&B Supervisor before declaring the count. Do not adjust without Supervisor present.

## Step 4 — Credit Card Totals Match

Each server verifies credit totals.

1. Compare Toast credit totals to the credit batch receipt from the terminal.
2. These should match to the penny — credit card settlement is an electronic match, not a count.
3. Any discrepancy is flagged to the Supervisor for investigation.

## Step 5 — Tip Declaration

Servers declare tips through Toast as part of the close-out.

1. Tips declared must include credit tips (captured automatically) plus cash tips (declared manually).
2. Cash tip declaration is expected to be accurate — under-declaring tips is both a compliance issue and, separately, a property issue.
3. Declared tip totals feed the payroll system automatically.

## Step 6 — Bartender Inventory Count

Bartenders complete an inventory verification at close that servers do not.

1. Count open bottles of premium spirits against the par sheet.
2. Count closed/back-of-house bottles of all spirits against the par sheet.
3. Note any bottle below par and record the shortfall on the nightly inventory log.
4. Clean and reset the bar per the Bar Closing SOP.

## Step 7 — F&B Supervisor Verification

Once each server has completed their close, the F&B Supervisor verifies.

1. Collect each server's close-out report, deposit bag, and any exception notes.
2. For each deposit bag, count the cash and match to the report total.
3. Sign the report as verified. The server signs beside the Supervisor.
4. Deposit bag is sealed and placed in the F&B cash safe.

## Step 8 — Shift-Level Reconciliation

With all individual closes complete, the F&B Supervisor closes the shift.

1. Print the Shift Sales Report from Toast.
2. Verify total shift revenue (food + beverage + other) reconciles to the sum of individual close-outs.
3. Verify total cash sales reconciles to total cash deposited.
4. Verify total credit sales reconciles to the credit batch.
5. Verify comp and void totals match any approval log for the shift.
6. Close the shift in Toast. This generates the final Shift Summary Report which routes to OPERA Cloud for posting in the night audit.

## Step 9 — Safe Deposit

The Supervisor deposits all sealed bags in the F&B cash safe.

1. Complete the deposit log at the safe — date, shift end, bag count, total cash amount, Supervisor initial.
2. A second staff member (typically the closing bartender) witnesses the deposit and counter-signs the log.
3. The deposit is collected the next morning by the GM or designated deposit runner for bank delivery.

## Variance Handling

Any cash variance is addressed immediately, not deferred.

### Variance Under $5

1. Recount both the server's deposit and the opening bank.
2. Verify no cash tips have been mixed with shift cash.
3. Verify any cash refund or cash-paid purchase during the shift was logged.
4. If the variance remains, document and accept. Minor variances at this level are expected operational noise.

### Variance $5 to $25

1. Recount. Verify opening bank and deposit.
2. Review the Toast close-out report for unusual voids, discount applications, or manual adjustments.
3. Document the variance and suspected cause in the Shift Incident Log.
4. Continue close-out. GM reviews the next morning.

### Variance Over $25

1. Do not accept. Recount twice.
2. Review every void and comp from the shift for proper authorization.
3. Review any cash refund or cash out transaction.
4. Notify the GM by cell phone before completing the close-out.
5. Document fully. Do not attribute blame without investigation.
6. Depending on GM direction, the shift may be closed with the variance logged, or additional review may be required before close.

## Comp and Void Authorization

Every comp and void creates a variance opportunity. The F&B Supervisor confirms at close that:

1. Every void has a reason code entered in Toast.
2. Every comp over $25 has a Supervisor authorization recorded in Toast.
3. Complimentary items (e.g., a dessert comp'd for a birthday) are entered as comp items, not voided from the ticket.

## Closing Checklist

Before leaving the property, the F&B Supervisor confirms:

- [ ] All tickets closed in Toast
- [ ] All server close-outs verified
- [ ] All cash deposited to the safe with log entry
- [ ] Shift closed in Toast
- [ ] Variance (if any) documented
- [ ] Bar inventory count complete
- [ ] POS terminals powered down to standby
- [ ] F&B cash safe locked
- [ ] Closing Checklist signed and filed

## Documentation

- **Toast Shift Summary Report** — archived automatically; accessible to GM and Accounting.
- **Deposit Log** — paper log at the safe.
- **Shift Incident Log** — shared document in the F&B drive.
- **Nightly Inventory Log** (bar) — paper log with bartender signature.
- **Closing Checklist** — paper form, signed and filed in the F&B office.

## Common Failure Modes

- Server close-out rushed at the terminal with cash uncounted — "I'll just count when I get home." Never acceptable.
- Supervisor verifies a close without a physical cash count — a signature without a count is worse than no signature.
- Comp entered as a void — makes the comp invisible on the comp report and inflates the void totals.
- Tip declaration rushed to match the exact declared amount — cash tips that are declared incorrectly are a compliance issue the Supervisor catches, not enables.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the GM by cell phone if:
- Cash variance exceeds $25
- Credit card batch does not reconcile to Toast credit totals
- Any ticket cannot be closed because of a system issue
- Any staff theft is suspected
