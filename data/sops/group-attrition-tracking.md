# Attrition Clause Tracking and Client Notification

**Property:** Ridgeline Lodge, Steamboat Springs, CO
**Department:** Group Management
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Attrition is the contractual minimum room pickup a group must achieve. If the group contracted 40 rooms with an 80% attrition clause, they are financially responsible for 32 rooms minimum — regardless of how many guests actually book. This procedure defines how Ridgeline Lodge tracks a group's pickup against their attrition threshold, notifies the client when they are trending short, and enforces the clause if necessary. The goal is to protect property revenue while giving the client every reasonable chance to hit their minimum without damaging the relationship.

## Scope

Applies to the General Manager (who functions as Revenue Manager at this property), Front Desk Agents, and Night Auditor. Covers the period from rooming list due date through group departure. Attrition enforcement is coordinated with ownership and legal where amounts are material.

## Responsibilities

- **General Manager:** Owns the attrition tracking process; communicates with the client; authorizes any deviation from the contracted clause.
- **Front Desk Agent / Night Auditor:** Monitors daily group pickup against the Group Master in OPERA Cloud; flags concerns to the GM.
- **Ownership:** Reviewed on any attrition claim exceeding $5,000 in liability.

## Understanding Attrition

Three terms that are frequently confused — understand each before touching a contract.

- **Block:** The total rooms contracted (e.g., 40 rooms × 3 nights = 120 room nights blocked).
- **Attrition Threshold:** The percentage of the block the group must pick up to avoid a charge (e.g., 80%).
- **Pickup:** The actual confirmed reservations tracking against the block.

In the example above, the group's attrition threshold is 80% of 120 room nights = 96 room nights. If actual pickup is 75 room nights at group cutoff, the group owes the contracted rate on the 21-room-night shortfall (96 minus 75). The exact dollar formula — room rate only, or room rate plus tax — is defined by the contract and must be read before any conversation with the client.

## Tracking Schedule

Group pickup is tracked and communicated on a defined cadence. Do not wait until the cutoff date to raise concerns.

| Checkpoint | When | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Contract milestone | 60 days before arrival | Review block and attrition clause; refresh GM's memory |
| Soft check-in | 45 days before arrival | Compare current pickup to block; flag if below 50% |
| Mid-point | 30 days before arrival | Formal pickup update to client |
| Cutoff minus 10 | 10 days before cutoff | Attrition risk assessment and client call if trending short |
| Cutoff date | Per contract (typically 21–30 days out) | Final pickup count; attrition determination |

## Step 1 — Block and Clause Review at 60 Days

1. Pull up the Group Master in OPERA Cloud's Groups module.
2. Confirm the contracted block total in room nights.
3. Confirm the attrition threshold percentage.
4. Calculate the minimum required pickup in room nights (block × attrition threshold).
5. Confirm the contracted cutoff date.
6. Document all four figures in the Group Notes field in OPERA Cloud. This is the reference the whole team uses for the remainder of tracking.

## Step 2 — Soft Check-in at 45 Days

1. Run the Group Pickup Report in OPERA Cloud for this group.
2. Compare current pickup (confirmed reservations) against the block.
3. Calculate pickup as a percentage: current pickup / block.
4. If pickup is below 50%, log a note for the GM to reach out to the client with a friendly check-in. Do not frame this as a warning — frame as support ("How can we help you promote the block?").
5. Offer promotional tools: block link, hotel description for the client's communication, expedited room assignment for early bookers.

## Step 3 — Formal Mid-Point Update at 30 Days

The General Manager calls or emails the client with the 30-day pickup status.

1. Run the Group Pickup Report.
2. Prepare a one-line status for the client: "As of today, you're at 62 of 120 room nights — on track toward the 96 room night minimum, assuming the normal cadence of late bookings."
3. If pickup is healthy (above 70% of the attrition threshold at this checkpoint), the message is informational and supportive.
4. If pickup is weak (below 50% of the attrition threshold), the message introduces the attrition clause in clear language. Do not refer to it obliquely. Example: "Your contract has an 80% attrition clause, which works out to 96 room nights. At the current pace, we want to flag that you'd be tracking below that threshold. Let's talk about how to close the gap."
5. Document the conversation in the Group Notes.

## Step 4 — Cutoff Minus 10: Risk Assessment

Ten days before the contracted cutoff date, the GM performs a formal attrition risk assessment.

1. Run the Group Pickup Report.
2. Calculate projected final pickup. Use the property's historical late-booking pace as a guide — at Ridgeline Lodge, ski-season groups typically add 10–15% in the final 10 days.
3. If projected final pickup is below the attrition threshold, prepare the Attrition Notification Letter (template in the shared drive).
4. Call the client first. Do not send the letter before the call. The sequence is: conversation first, written confirmation second.

### Conducting the Attrition Call

1. State the facts. "Right now you have 75 of the 96 required room nights. Based on historical booking patterns, we project you'd finish around 85 — about 11 room nights short."
2. State the financial implication. Reference the specific dollar figure per the contract.
3. Offer solutions the property can support:
   - Extend the cutoff by 7 days if it buys meaningful additional pickup.
   - Release a portion of the block back to general inventory if the group can realistically pick it up.
   - Substitute a different room type if the shortfall is a mix issue.
4. Document the call in the Group Notes with date, time, client contact, and agreed-upon next step.

## Step 5 — Cutoff Date: Final Determination

On the contracted cutoff date:

1. Run the final Group Pickup Report at the end of the business day.
2. Compare final pickup to the attrition threshold.
3. If pickup meets or exceeds the threshold, email the client confirming no attrition charge applies.
4. If pickup is below the threshold, calculate the shortfall and the associated charge per the contract formula.
5. Any attrition invoice over $5,000 is reviewed by ownership before it is sent. Do not send unilaterally.
6. Send the attrition invoice with a copy of the contract clause and the pickup report as backup.

## Documentation

- **Group Master — Group Notes field in OPERA Cloud:** Running log of all pickup tracking, client communications, and decisions.
- **Attrition Notification Letter:** Template in the shared drive; filed in the group's folder when used.
- **Group Pickup Report snapshots:** Captured at each checkpoint and filed in the group's folder.
- **Final attrition determination email:** Sent to client and copied to ownership.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact ownership if:
- Projected attrition charge exceeds $5,000
- Client disputes the clause or refuses to acknowledge the communication
- Any threat of legal action
- Client requests a waiver of the attrition clause as a concession

## Common Mistakes

- Waiting until the cutoff date to raise attrition concerns — the client feels ambushed and the relationship takes a hit even when the property is legally in the right.
- Running pickup numbers but not reading the contract formula — some contracts charge on room rate only, others on room rate plus tax, others on a sliding scale.
- Offering a concession over the phone without authority — any waiver or reduction is authorized by ownership.
