# Shift Handover and Pass-On Log

**Property:** The Pelican House, Destin, FL
**Department:** Front Office
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Shift handover is the single most reliable prevention against guest-impacting errors at The Pelican House. This procedure governs how outgoing Front Desk Agents transfer operational status, open guest issues, and pending actions to the incoming shift. A shift ends only when the handover is complete — not at the scheduled end time.

## Scope

Applies to all Front Desk Agents and Night Auditors. Covers morning-to-afternoon, afternoon-to-overnight, and overnight-to-morning handovers. The General Manager reviews the Pass-On Log daily during the morning operations check-in.

## Responsibilities

- **Outgoing Front Desk Agent:** Completes the Pass-On Log before leaving; verbally walks the incoming agent through all open items; does not clock out until handover is acknowledged.
- **Incoming Front Desk Agent:** Reviews the Pass-On Log on arrival; asks clarifying questions; acknowledges receipt by initialing the log.
- **Night Auditor:** Performs outgoing-shift handover at 7:00 AM; reviews the Housekeeping dispatch board before handing off.
- **Manager on Duty:** Reviews the Pass-On Log during the morning operations check-in and flags any items requiring follow-up.

## The Pass-On Log

The Pass-On Log is maintained as a shared document in the Mews Shift Notes feature. Each entry is time-stamped and attributed to the agent who logged it. Paper notes are not acceptable — all handover items must live in Mews so they are retrievable across shifts and by management.

### Required Entries

Before handover, the outgoing agent logs every item in each category below. If a category has no open items, note "None."

1. **Arrivals still pending:** Reservations expected but not yet checked in; include expected arrival time if known.
2. **Departures still pending:** Guests expected to check out who have not yet done so; note any approved late checkouts.
3. **Out-of-order rooms:** Current OOO status and expected return date; reference the Out-of-Order Room Reporting SOP.
4. **Open guest issues:** Any complaint, comp, or service recovery in progress; include Manager involvement if any.
5. **VIP or special-attention guests:** In-house VIPs, special occasions, return guest recognition notes.
6. **Group activity:** Group arrivals or departures in progress; reference group master folio and contact.
7. **Maintenance requests:** Pending work orders and expected response times.
8. **Cash / float variance:** Any discrepancy in the drawer at shift end, documented and initialed.

## Handover Sequence

The outgoing and incoming agents complete the following at shift change:

1. Outgoing agent completes all Pass-On Log entries before the scheduled end of shift.
2. The two agents stand together at the desk with Mews open. Outgoing agent walks through every open item in the log aloud.
3. Incoming agent asks questions until every item is clear. No assumption is allowed to pass — if a detail is unclear, it gets clarified.
4. Incoming agent physically counts the shift float in the drawer and confirms it matches the outgoing total.
5. Incoming agent initials the Pass-On Log to acknowledge receipt.
6. Outgoing agent logs out of Mews and hands over any property keys or radio.
7. Only after these steps is the outgoing agent released from shift.

## Handover Timing

- **Morning to afternoon:** 3:00 PM, with a 15-minute paid overlap built into the schedule.
- **Afternoon to overnight:** 11:00 PM, with a 15-minute paid overlap.
- **Overnight to morning:** 7:00 AM, with a 15-minute paid overlap. The Night Auditor is expected to stay until handover is complete even if this runs past scheduled end time.

## Housekeeping Coordination at Handover

At each handover, the outgoing and incoming agents confirm the current state of the Housekeeping dispatch board:

1. Review the Housekeeping tab in Mews for rooms in progress and expected completion times.
2. Identify any expected early arrivals whose rooms are not yet clean.
3. Flag any rooms returned to Out-of-Order status during the shift.
4. Communicate any guest requests that require Housekeeping follow-up (extra pillows, crib, turndown).

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the Manager on Duty if:
- A Pass-On Log entry cannot be resolved during the handover conversation
- The shift float does not reconcile within $10
- An open guest issue requires immediate action that the incoming shift cannot execute alone
- An agent does not show up for shift and coverage cannot be secured

## Common Failure Modes

- Entries logged as "see previous shift" with no actual detail — every entry must be self-contained.
- Verbal handover skipped because the log is "all there" — the spoken walkthrough catches what writing misses.
- Float count rushed and signed off without a physical count.
