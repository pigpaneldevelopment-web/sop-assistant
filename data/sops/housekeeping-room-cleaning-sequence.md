# Room Cleaning Sequence and Standards

**Property:** Ridgeline Lodge, Steamboat Springs, CO
**Department:** Housekeeping
**Document Owner:** Director of Housekeeping
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

This procedure defines the sequence and standards for cleaning a guest room at Ridgeline Lodge from entry to inspection. The standard produces a consistently clean, quiet, and ski-resort-appropriate room in the time budget assigned. Because our guests often arrive covered in snow and depart at varying times, efficiency and accuracy under pressure matter equally.

## Scope

Applies to all Room Attendants and Inspectors. Covers both departure cleans (full turnover) and stay-over cleans (refresh). Time standards below apply to a standard king or queen guest room. Suite cleaning standards are covered in a separate SOP.

## Responsibilities

- **Room Attendant:** Cleans rooms to standard in the sequence and time allowance defined below; logs completion in HotSOS via tablet.
- **Inspector:** Verifies every departure clean before the room is released to the Front Desk; signs off in OPERA Cloud Housekeeping module.
- **Director of Housekeeping:** Audits 10% of daily cleans; resolves board disputes; adjusts daily assignments based on early arrivals.

## Tools and Supplies

Before starting the shift, the Room Attendant confirms the following in the cart:

- Microfiber cloths (color-coded: blue for glass, green for bathroom, yellow for general)
- Bathroom cleaner, multi-surface cleaner, glass cleaner
- Vacuum, mop, bucket
- Bed linens, towels, and bathroom amenities sized to the day's room count
- Tablet logged into HotSOS

If any tool or supply is missing, notify the Director of Housekeeping before leaving the linen room. Do not start a room short on supplies.

## Entry Procedure

1. Review the daily assignment on the HotSOS tablet. Confirm the room is not marked "Do Not Disturb."
2. Approach the door. Knock firmly three times and announce "Housekeeping."
3. Wait 10 seconds. Knock and announce again.
4. After a third knock with no response, enter with the master key. Prop the door open with the door stop — never with the room's own door hardware.
5. Update the room status in HotSOS to "In Progress" before starting work.
6. If the guest is present, apologize and offer to return later. Log the return time in HotSOS and move to the next assigned room.

## Departure Clean Sequence

A full departure clean is budgeted at 30 minutes for a king/queen room. Follow this sequence exactly — it exists for efficiency and inspection consistency.

### Initial Walkthrough (3 minutes)

1. Open blackout curtains and window for air circulation. Winter months: open window no longer than 5 minutes.
2. Empty all trash. Check the desk, bathroom, and any wastebaskets.
3. Gather all used linens and towels into the soiled linen bag.
4. Scan for left-behind items. Any found item is handled per the Lost and Found SOP.
5. Flush all toilets and start the bathroom cleaning products soaking while you complete other tasks.

### Bathroom (10 minutes)

1. Clean the toilet — interior, exterior, base, and floor behind.
2. Clean the tub/shower — walls, fixtures, drain. Check for hair in the drain.
3. Clean the vanity — mirror, sink, counter, fixtures. Polish chrome.
4. Replace all linens, amenities, and tissue/toilet paper. All rolls folded to a point.
5. Mop bathroom floor last, working toward the door.

### Bedroom (12 minutes)

1. Make the bed. Crisp corners; duvet symmetric; pillows fluffed and placed per property standard.
2. Dust all horizontal surfaces — nightstands, dresser, desk, headboard, picture frames, lamps.
3. Clean the interior of the coffee station. Replace supplies to par.
4. Wipe down the phone, remote, thermostat, and all switches.
5. Polish the mirror and any glass surfaces.

### Final (5 minutes)

1. Vacuum the entire carpet, working toward the door. Vacuum lines should be visible.
2. Replace the "Clean Room" signage per property standard.
3. Close the window and restore curtains.
4. Set thermostat to 68°F (winter) or 72°F (summer).
5. Update room status in HotSOS to "Clean — Awaiting Inspection."

## Stay-Over Clean Sequence

A stay-over clean is budgeted at 15 minutes. The goal is a refreshed room, not a full turnover.

1. Knock, announce, and enter per the Entry Procedure.
2. Empty trash and replace liners.
3. Make the bed to property standard — if the guest has clearly slept on top, fold the duvet at the foot rather than stripping.
4. Refresh towels. Replace only towels left on the floor or in the tub. Hanging towels remain unless visibly soiled.
5. Replace amenities to par.
6. Quick wipe of bathroom surfaces. Full toilet, tub, and floor clean only on departure.
7. Vacuum only if visibly needed.
8. Respect the guest's belongings. Do not move personal items; clean around them.

## Inspection Handoff

The Room Attendant does not release the room. After marking "Clean — Awaiting Inspection," the Inspector performs the following:

1. Review against the Inspection Checklist on the HotSOS tablet.
2. Spot-check bathroom, bed corners, dust on high surfaces, and under the bed.
3. If the room fails inspection, return it to the Room Attendant with specific notes. Do not re-clean silently.
4. When the room passes, update status to "Clean and Inspected" in OPERA Cloud. The room is now available for front office assignment.

## Escalation Triggers

Notify the Director of Housekeeping if:
- A room shows damage beyond normal wear
- A left-behind item appears valuable (electronics, jewelry, wallet, medication)
- The guest is present and refuses service after a second attempt
- The room shows evidence of smoking, pets without authorization, or parties
