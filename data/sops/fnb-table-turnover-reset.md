# Table Turnover and Reset Standards

**Property:** Salinas Point Inn, Central California Coast
**Department:** Food & Beverage
**Document Owner:** F&B Supervisor
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Table turnover at Salinas Point Inn's restaurant is where the service experience meets the operational math. A well-executed reset gets a clean, set, guest-ready table in under 4 minutes while maintaining the polish the property is known for. This procedure defines the sequence, standards, and coordination across Server, Busser, and F&B Supervisor that keeps the dining room moving without feeling rushed.

## Scope

Applies to Servers, Bussers, and the F&B Supervisor working in the main dining room. Covers standard two-top and four-top resets, large-top resets (6+), patio seating resets, and bar seating resets. Does not cover banquet table resets for private events.

## Responsibilities

- **Server:** Owns the relationship with the departing table through the payment close; signals turnover readiness to the Busser.
- **Busser:** Executes the clear-and-reset sequence; restocks the table; signals ready to the F&B Supervisor or Host.
- **F&B Supervisor / Host:** Verifies the reset meets standard before seating the next party; times the reset against the target window.

## The 4-Minute Standard

Target turnover time at Salinas Point Inn is 4 minutes from guest departure to next-party-ready. Turnovers exceeding 7 minutes impact seating flow during peak service. The standard assumes two-person execution (Server + Busser). A turnover handled by one person is allowed 6 minutes.

### Why Not Faster

Faster is not always better. A 2-minute reset looks frantic from across the dining room and tells waiting guests they are watching a production line. The 4-minute target produces a polished reset without compromising the experience for the guests seated around the turning table.

## The Close-Out Flow

The turnover sequence begins before the guest stands up. A good reset is set up by a good close-out.

1. Server presents the check no earlier than the guest has signaled readiness (asked for it, finished coffee or last course) and no later than 2 minutes after that signal.
2. Server processes the payment via the Toast handheld. Do not leave the table with the check and return — present, process, and close in one trip.
3. Server thanks the guest, escorts them through the dining room if appropriate (for regulars, special occasions, or VIPs), and returns to the table.

## The Reset Sequence

### Step 1 — Initial Clear (60 seconds)

Server and Busser work the table together for the first minute.

1. Server removes all glassware, cups, and drinkware to a hands-only tray. Do not stack glassware.
2. Busser removes all china, flatware, and napkins to a plate-return bus tub.
3. Both remove any remaining bread service, condiments, butter.
4. Server removes any remaining menus, card holders, and check presenters.
5. Busser wipes down the table with a clean cloth and sanitizer from the service station caddy.

### Step 2 — Reset (2 minutes)

Busser owns the reset. Server returns to active tables.

1. Replace tablecloth if used (pull soiled to the linen return bin; set fresh cloth centered).
2. Place chargers at each seat per property standard.
3. Fold napkin to property standard (simple fold — not an origami sculpture) and place to the right of the charger.
4. Set flatware: salad fork, entree fork on napkin left; entree knife (blade toward plate), teaspoon on right.
5. Place water glass upper right of charger; wine glass to right of water glass at appropriate angle.
6. Replace any service props — fresh flower, fresh candle, clean condiment caddy.
7. Position chairs tucked to the table edge, not pushed in hard.

### Step 3 — Final Inspection (60 seconds)

1. Busser visually scans the table for symmetry — flatware aligned, glassware upright, napkin flat.
2. Busser wipes any stray crumbs from chairs.
3. Busser signals the F&B Supervisor or Host that the table is ready.
4. F&B Supervisor or Host performs a visual check from across the dining room — a set that looks right from 10 feet away is usually right close-up.

## Large-Top Resets (6+)

Large tops have more variables and require coordination.

1. Server notifies the F&B Supervisor 10 minutes before projected departure. Supervisor assigns a second Busser to assist.
2. Two Bussers execute the reset in parallel. Target time: 6 minutes for six tops, 8 minutes for eights.
3. Any large-top reset that exceeds 10 minutes is flagged to the Supervisor for coaching.

## Patio Seating Resets

The patio at Salinas Point Inn is subject to wind off the Pacific. Resets require additional steps:

1. Weight napkins with a clean napkin ring — paper napkins or unweighted linen blow off the table.
2. Secure umbrellas before reset if wind is present.
3. Do not set tables more than one turn in advance during windy conditions.
4. Outdoor flatware warms in direct sun; wipe with a clean cloth to cool before placement.
5. In fog or mist, set tables one turn at a time — flatware oxidizes with moisture exposure.

## Bar Seating Resets

Bar seat resets are simplified but still owned by the Bartender or assigned Server:

1. Clear all glassware, napkins, and snack ramekins.
2. Wipe the bar top with sanitizer.
3. Reset with a fresh cocktail napkin at each seat.
4. Restock any service garnish within reach.

## Coordination with the Kitchen

When a table is reset, the kitchen is not always aware that a new party is incoming. The Host or F&B Supervisor seats the next party and inputs the cover count into Toast, which signals the kitchen. The Server approaches within 2 minutes of seating to take drink orders.

## Common Failure Modes

- Server takes the check back for the card and forgets to return promptly — the guest sits at a closed-out table for 4 minutes, the Server feels justified speeding through the reset, and two bad impressions happen in one turnover.
- Busser wipes the table but misses the seat cushions or the table base; next guest sits in crumbs.
- Flatware placed on a damp table shows water streaks immediately; dry the table fully before resetting.
- Reset looks correct but the water glass is chipped or the napkin has a stain — a fast reset with dirty china is worse than a slow reset.

## Service Quality Standards

A table is not "reset" until it passes the standard. The F&B Supervisor is authorized and expected to reject a reset that doesn't meet standard. Rejection is communicated privately to the Busser — never in front of guests.

## Documentation

- Reset times are not individually logged. However, the F&B Supervisor tracks observed turnover speed during service and discusses trends in post-shift standup.
- Any turnover exceeding 10 minutes in a two-person reset is noted for coaching.
