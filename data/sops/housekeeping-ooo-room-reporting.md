# Out-of-Order Room Reporting and Tracking

**Property:** The Cannon & Vine, Nashville, TN
**Department:** Housekeeping
**Document Owner:** Director of Housekeeping
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

An out-of-order (OOO) room is inventory the property cannot sell. Every OOO night is lost revenue. This procedure defines how rooms are identified, reported, tracked, returned to service, and audited at The Cannon & Vine. The program exists to minimize OOO duration without compromising guest experience when a room is legitimately not ready to sell.

## Scope

Applies to all Room Attendants, Inspectors, the Director of Housekeeping, the Maintenance Engineer, and the Front Office team. Covers all guest rooms, suites, and common area flagged issues that require room removal from inventory.

## Responsibilities

- **Room Attendant:** Identifies and reports any condition requiring OOO status; does not mark the room OOO without Inspector or Director of Housekeeping confirmation.
- **Inspector:** Verifies reported conditions; can authorize short-term OOO for same-day resolution.
- **Director of Housekeeping:** Authorizes all multi-day OOO; manages the OOO log; coordinates return-to-service.
- **Maintenance Engineer:** Receives the work order; provides time estimates; performs repairs; confirms completion.
- **General Manager:** Reviews OOO log weekly; authorizes OOO that exceeds 7 days.

## OOO Categories

Rooms are flagged OOO for one of the following reasons. Each carries different handling.

### Category 1 — Maintenance (Minor)

Issues resolvable same-day: burned-out bulb fixture replacement, thermostat calibration, minor plumbing leak, TV replacement, broken drawer hardware.

### Category 2 — Maintenance (Major)

Issues requiring extended repair: HVAC unit replacement, flooring repair or replacement, plumbing requiring wall access, smoke damage remediation, pest treatment requiring dwell time.

### Category 3 — Deep Clean

Rooms returning to service after smoke incident, excessive soiling, allergen issue, or routine deep-clean rotation.

### Category 4 — Refurbishment

Planned work: soft goods replacement, paint, carpet replacement, bathroom updates. Scheduled by the GM with ownership.

### Category 5 — Pest Control

Confirmed or suspected pest activity — bed bugs, rodents, etc. Handled with elevated scrutiny per the Pest Control section below.

## Reporting Sequence

### When a Room Attendant Discovers an Issue

1. Do not mark the room OOO on the tablet. Room Attendants identify conditions; authorization comes from the Inspector or Director.
2. Complete any cleaning work that can be completed without interfering with the issue (e.g., if the HVAC is broken, still clean the bathroom and change linens while the room is vacant).
3. Document the issue in HotSOS with a photograph.
4. Notify the Inspector. If the Inspector is not on shift, notify the Director of Housekeeping directly via the shared team SMS.
5. Continue to the next assigned room.

### When the Inspector Evaluates

1. Inspector visits the room within 30 minutes of the report.
2. Confirm the issue and assess category.
3. For Category 1 (same-day resolvable), create a work order in the Maintenance module of Cloudbeds. Authorize short-term OOO (up to 4 hours) by marking the room "OOO — Short" in Cloudbeds.
4. For Category 2–5, notify the Director of Housekeeping before marking the room OOO. These require Director authorization.

### When the Director Authorizes

1. Director reviews the issue, either in person or by photo from HotSOS.
2. Director marks the room "OOO" in Cloudbeds with a category tag and a projected return-to-service date.
3. Director creates or elevates the Maintenance work order.
4. Director notifies the Front Office via the Cloudbeds team message so the room is not blocked for an incoming guest.
5. Director adds the room to the OOO Log (shared spreadsheet in the Housekeeping drive).

## The OOO Log

The OOO Log is the central record. Every entry contains:

- Date room went OOO
- Room number
- Category
- Issue description
- Work order number
- Projected return-to-service date
- Actual return-to-service date
- Director sign-off initial

The log is updated in real time — not retroactively. Directors who batch-update the log at end of day are failing the procedure.

## Return to Service

A room does not come back into inventory automatically. Return requires an explicit sequence.

1. Maintenance Engineer notifies the Director when work is complete.
2. Director or Inspector performs a full re-inspection. This is not a normal post-clean inspection — it is an OOO return inspection with elevated scrutiny.
3. For Category 2 or Category 3 rooms, the Inspector cleans the room to departure-clean standards before the return inspection, even if no guest occupied the room.
4. If the inspection passes, the Director marks the room "Clean and Inspected" in Cloudbeds.
5. The Front Office receives the team message that the room is back in inventory.
6. The Director updates the OOO Log with the actual return date.

## Pest Control Handling

Category 5 rooms require elevated handling. Bed bug or rodent reports carry legal and reputational weight and must be managed carefully.

1. Upon any suspected pest activity, the room is immediately removed from inventory by the Inspector — do not wait for Director authorization.
2. The adjacent rooms (both sides, above, below) are inspected within 2 hours. If any evidence is found, those rooms are also removed from inventory.
3. The General Manager is notified immediately by phone.
4. A licensed pest control vendor is contacted the same day.
5. Treatment follows the vendor's protocol, including dwell time. Do not shortcut dwell time to return the room to inventory.
6. Deep clean is performed after treatment. Linens and upholstery are replaced or professionally treated per vendor guidance.
7. A follow-up inspection by the vendor confirms clearance before the room returns to inventory.
8. Any guest who occupied the room prior to the report is flagged in the Guest Card. The GM handles communication to the guest personally — never the FDA.

## Weekly Review

The Director of Housekeeping and General Manager review the OOO Log weekly.

1. Total OOO nights for the week.
2. Average OOO duration by category.
3. Repeat-offender rooms — any room OOO more than once in 30 days is flagged for root-cause review.
4. Aging entries — any room OOO longer than 7 days requires a GM plan to close or escalate to ownership.

## Documentation

- **HotSOS entries** per room and issue.
- **Cloudbeds OOO status** on each room record.
- **OOO Log** in the Housekeeping shared drive.
- **Maintenance work orders** linked to each entry.
- **Pest control vendor reports** for any Category 5 entry, filed in the GM's office.

## Escalation Triggers

Notify the General Manager if:
- A room has been OOO longer than 7 days
- Category 5 activity is suspected or confirmed
- OOO nights for the week exceed 2% of total room nights
- The Maintenance Engineer cannot resolve a Category 1 issue within 4 hours
