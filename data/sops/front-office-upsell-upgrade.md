# Upsell and Room Upgrade Procedure

**Property:** Juniper Creek Ranch, Fredericksburg, TX
**Department:** Front Office
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Upsell at Juniper Creek Ranch is not about hitting a revenue target — it is about offering the guest a better experience and giving them the chance to say yes. When done well, upsells produce genuine guest satisfaction and material incremental revenue; when done poorly, they feel like a sales pitch at the worst possible moment. This procedure defines how Front Desk Agents identify opportunities, offer them, close them, and track them.

## Scope

Applies to all Front Desk Agents at arrival. Covers room type upgrades, early check-in, late check-out (when paid), and property add-ons (spa, horseback, tasting flights at the on-site tasting room). Does not cover F&B upsell in the restaurant or bar — that is covered under F&B service standards.

## Responsibilities

- **Front Desk Agent:** Reviews daily upsell opportunities at shift start; makes the offer at check-in; processes accepted upgrades in Maestro; logs conversion.
- **Manager / General Manager:** Sets daily upsell offer rates; reviews weekly conversion reports; coaches FDA offer language.
- **Revenue Manager (GM functions):** Determines which upgrades are authorized to offer based on daily forecast.

## Philosophy

The property tier matters. Juniper Creek Ranch is boutique/upscale and independent — not a points-chasing environment, not a Ritz, not a select-service property. Guests who chose this property are generally willing to spend for experience. The upsell conversation should feel like a concierge making a suggestion, not a salesperson running a pitch.

- Frame the upgrade around the experience, not the feature list.
- Never apologize for the price. Quote the delta calmly.
- Never pressure. One offer, one clean ask, one clear response.
- A "no" is not a failed conversation. A rushed guest who feels sold-to becomes a bad review.

## Daily Preparation

At shift start, the Front Desk Agent reviews today's upsell opportunity.

1. Open the Maestro Upsell Dashboard. The Revenue Manager has flagged which room types have upgrade inventory available today and at what offer price.
2. Review the offer rate for each upgrade. Offer rates are typically set at 40–60% of the rack-to-rack delta.
3. Note any arrivals already flagged in the Guest Card for a suggested upsell (anniversary, return guest, special occasion).
4. Review property add-ons available today: spa openings, any horseback trail ride slots, any Texas Hill Country wine tasting flights in the tasting room.

## Identifying the Right Opportunity

Not every arriving guest is the right candidate for an upsell offer. Read the context before speaking.

- **Strong candidates:** Couples celebrating something, return guests, guests on a longer stay (3+ nights), guests who booked a base-level room and were the first to book at that rate (often priced-down bookers who might step up).
- **Weak candidates:** Guests who explicitly booked the suite already (nothing to upgrade to), guests checking in after a long drive who look exhausted, guests arriving in a group block with pre-set expectations.
- **Do not offer to:** Guests who are visibly stressed at check-in, guests with a complaint from their last stay, guests who arrive after a travel disruption.

## The Offer Sequence

Offers are made after the standard check-in sequence reaches the rate confirmation step — not before.

1. Complete the standard check-in sequence through ID verification and payment authorization.
2. Acknowledge the guest's existing reservation. "We've got you in the standard king for three nights."
3. Make the offer naturally. "I wanted to mention — we do have one of our Hill Country Suites open tonight, and I can upgrade you for $85 a night. It has a private patio overlooking the creek and a soaking tub if that sounds interesting."
4. Pause. Let the guest think.
5. If the guest accepts, process per the Acceptance Sequence below.
6. If the guest declines, respond graciously. "No problem at all — you'll love the king room. Let me know if you need anything during your stay." Do not counter-offer. Do not re-pitch.
7. If the guest hesitates with a question ("what's the difference?"), answer specifically and pause again.
8. Do not layer a second upsell onto a declined one ("well, how about…"). One offer is the rule.

### Offer Language That Works

- *Say:* "We have a creekside suite open if you'd like to upgrade for your anniversary — it's an extra $95 a night and comes with a private patio."
- *Say:* "Since you're here for three nights, I could move you to our King Hill View for $60 more total — you'd have a much bigger window and a separate sitting area."
- *Say:* "I noticed this is your third visit — we have a suite open tonight and I can offer it to you at $75 rather than the usual $120 upgrade."

### Language That Doesn't Fit This Tier

- *Avoid:* "Would you be interested in upgrading today?" (Generic, script-feeling.)
- *Avoid:* "For only $95 more per night, you could…" (Retail voice.)
- *Avoid:* "Are you sure? It's a really nice room." (Pressure after a decline.)

## Acceptance Sequence

When the guest says yes:

1. Confirm the new room type and the total incremental cost. "So we'll move you to the Hill Country Suite at $85 a night for three nights — an additional $255 plus tax. Good with that?"
2. Update the reservation in Maestro. Change the room type and the rate. Verify the new rate is loaded correctly.
3. Re-authorize the incidental hold and the updated room total.
4. Reassign the specific room in the Room Rack. Confirm the new room is "Clean and Inspected."
5. Re-key to the new room number.
6. Briefly orient the guest to any amenity specific to the upgraded room.
7. Log the conversion in the Upsell Log with the offer amount, acceptance, and the FDA's initials.

## Property Add-On Sales

Beyond room upgrades, the FDA is authorized to book the following at check-in:

- **Spa appointments:** Book through the spa's scheduler; confirm in the Guest Card.
- **Horseback trail rides:** Book through the ranch activities system; confirm in the Guest Card.
- **Wine tasting flight:** Reserve through the tasting room's OpenTable-style booking.
- **In-room amenity add-ons:** Flower arrangement, champagne, cheese board — order through Maestro Guest Services Module.

Frame these as suggestions, not pitches. "We have a sunset trail ride slot open tomorrow at 5:30 — shall I hold it for you?"

## Offer Rate Authority

- FDA may offer the posted upsell rate without Manager approval.
- FDA may negotiate up to 10% below the posted offer rate for close-the-deal conversations. Above that requires Manager approval.
- Complimentary upgrades are never given as part of upsell conversion — comps are handled under service recovery or VIP programs, not upsell.

## Tracking and Weekly Review

Every offer and outcome is logged, not just the wins.

1. The Upsell Log (shared document) captures: date, FDA initials, guest name/room, offer made, offer amount, result (accepted / declined / no offer).
2. "No offer" entries matter — they surface arrivals where the FDA didn't attempt the sequence and inform coaching.
3. The GM reviews the log weekly. Conversion rate per FDA is discussed in one-on-ones, not broadcast.

## Common Mistakes

- Offering the upgrade before the guest's existing reservation is confirmed — feels like a bait-and-switch.
- Pressing after a decline — the second ask kills the relationship.
- Quoting the rack-to-rack delta instead of the actual offer price — makes the upgrade feel more expensive than it is.
- Forgetting to update the reservation in Maestro after a verbal acceptance — results in the guest being charged the original rate at departure.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the Manager if:
- The guest requests an upgrade rate below the FDA's 10% authority
- The guest requests a complimentary upgrade
- An upgraded reservation was accepted but the target room is unavailable at check-in
- A guest disputes an upsell charge at departure
