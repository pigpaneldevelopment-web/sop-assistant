# Departure vs. Stay-Over Room Prioritization

**Property:** The Ironwood, Sedona, AZ
**Department:** Housekeeping
**Document Owner:** Director of Housekeeping
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

The daily cleaning plan at The Ironwood has to balance two competing pressures: departures must be cleaned and inspected before incoming arrivals need them, and stayovers must be serviced before the guest returns in the afternoon. This procedure defines how departures and stayovers are prioritized, sequenced, and assigned, and how the plan adjusts when early arrivals, late check-outs, or staffing issues change the math.

## Scope

Applies to the Director of Housekeeping, Inspectors, Room Attendants, and the Front Office team that coordinates the daily room readiness plan. Covers the full 6:45 AM to 4:00 PM housekeeping shift window.

## Responsibilities

- **Director of Housekeeping:** Builds the daily cleaning plan; assigns rooms to Room Attendants; adjusts the plan in real time based on arrival and departure changes.
- **Inspector:** Verifies rooms in the inspected-first priority order; signals the Front Office through OPERA Cloud when departure rooms are ready.
- **Room Attendant:** Works assigned rooms in the sequence directed; communicates any delay to the Inspector or Director.
- **Front Desk Agent:** Coordinates early arrival and late departure requests with the Director in real time; does not promise rooms by specific times without checking.

## The Core Priority Rule

On any day with departures and stayovers, the order of priority is:

1. **Pre-assigned departure rooms for early arrivals or VIP arrivals** — inspected by 11:00 AM target.
2. **General departure rooms needed for same-day arrivals** — inspected by 3:00 PM standard check-in time.
3. **Stayover rooms for guests who have requested early service** — serviced before the requested time.
4. **Standard stayover rooms** — serviced between 10:00 AM and 3:00 PM, guest preferences respected (DND signs, window cards).
5. **Departure rooms not needed for arrivals tonight** — cleaned and held for inventory flexibility.

The priority order is a default. Specific exceptions override — a VIP stayover may be prioritized ahead of a departure; a deep-clean departure rotation may take longer than a standard one.

## Morning Plan Build (6:45 AM)

The Director of Housekeeping builds the day's cleaning plan at the start of shift.

1. Open the Housekeeping module in OPERA Cloud. Pull the Arrivals report for the day.
2. Pull the Departures report. Note any approved late checkouts and their cleared times.
3. Pull the Stayovers list. Identify any stayover with a service request note in the Guest Card.
4. Run the Room Status report. Confirm the current state of every room against what's expected.
5. Identify early arrival requests flagged by the Front Office (typically emailed or noted in the Guest Card the night before).
6. Identify VIP arrivals. Review their pre-assigned rooms.
7. Build the assignment sheet per Room Attendant. Target 14–16 rooms per RA for a standard mix of stayovers and departures; 12 for a departure-heavy day.
8. Sequence each RA's list by priority — first few rooms are early-arrival targets or VIP prep; middle rooms are stayover rotation; end of shift is held-inventory departures.

## Early Arrival Coordination

The Front Office is the source of truth for early arrival requests. By 7:00 AM, the Director confirms:

1. Which reservations have requested early arrival (typically flagged in the Guest Card).
2. Which of those reservations' rooms are currently occupied (therefore departures that must be cleaned before the guest arrives).
3. Which rooms are already clean and inspected and can be reassigned to early arrivals.

For each early-arrival target:

1. Mark the room on the assignment sheet as "EA — [time]."
2. Assign to a senior Room Attendant capable of the speed required.
3. Notify the Inspector that this room is a priority inspection.
4. Confirm with the Front Office the realistic ready time by 9:00 AM. The Front Office does not promise a guest a specific time until the Housekeeping confirmation comes back.

## Stayover Flow

Stayover rooms are serviced between 10:00 AM and 3:00 PM by default.

1. RAs begin the stayover rotation at 10:00 AM on their assigned floors.
2. Do Not Disturb signs are respected. If DND is active at 3:00 PM, the RA leaves a courtesy card noting the service was skipped and the option to call the desk for service.
3. "Service Now" cards (hanging from the door) are honored immediately upon rotation reaching that floor.
4. "Service Later" or specific time requests (captured at check-in or in the Guest Card) are honored within 30 minutes of the requested time.
5. Stayovers flagged for early departure the next day receive a full departure clean on the morning of departure — they are not serviced as stayovers the evening before.

## Mid-Day Plan Adjustments

The plan is not fixed at 6:45 AM. Adjustments happen throughout the morning.

### New Early Arrival Request Mid-Day

1. Front Desk Agent notifies the Director directly.
2. Director reviews the assignment sheet for the room and determines current status.
3. If the room is already in progress, confirm expected completion and communicate to FDA.
4. If the room has not been started, re-sequence the affected RA's assignment to prioritize the room.
5. Do not commit to a ready time until the room is actually clean and in the inspection queue.

### Late Check-Out Approved

1. When a late check-out is approved by the Front Desk, the FDA flags the room in OPERA Cloud with the updated departure time.
2. Director adjusts the assignment — moves the room to a later slot on the RA's list or reassigns to a different RA if the timing creates conflict.
3. If the late check-out is incompatible with an incoming early arrival for the same room, Front Desk and Director discuss alternate room options for the early arrival.

### Room Returns to OOO

1. If a departure clean reveals a condition requiring OOO (per Out-of-Order Room Reporting SOP), the room leaves inventory.
2. Director notifies the Front Desk immediately so the incoming arrival is reassigned.
3. Director identifies the next-best available clean room for the reassignment and communicates to the FDA.

## The 11:00 AM Checkpoint

A short checkpoint between Director and Inspectors at 11:00 AM catches most day-of issues.

1. Confirm all early arrival targets are complete or in inspection queue.
2. Count departures cleaned vs. departures remaining.
3. Identify any stayover that hasn't been attempted because of DND and plan a second-pass attempt.
4. Flag any RA who is behind on their assignment and determine whether to reassign rooms.

## The 2:00 PM Checkpoint

A final checkpoint at 2:00 PM focuses on the 3:00 PM arrival wave.

1. Confirm all rooms needed for 3:00 PM arrivals are clean and in the inspection queue.
2. Identify any room still in progress and communicate realistic ready time to the Front Office.
3. Address any stayover not yet serviced. Decide: attempt again or leave service note at the door.

## Common Failure Modes

- Over-promising early arrival times before checking inventory — Front Desk tells a guest "11 AM" because the room number is assigned, but the departure hasn't been inspected yet.
- Skipping the 11:00 AM checkpoint on a busy day — "we're too busy to meet" is exactly when the checkpoint saves time.
- Treating stayovers as filler work — a missed stayover is a failed guest touchpoint, not a deferred task.
- Sequencing RAs by floor convenience rather than priority — a neat geographic route doesn't help if the early-arrival room is done last.

## Staffing Shortage Handling

If an RA calls out and the plan cannot absorb the rooms:

1. Director redistributes the absent RA's assignment across remaining team.
2. Extend the shift for available RAs with overtime approval from the GM.
3. Reduce stayover scope — communicate to Front Office that stayovers may run later than standard.
4. Prioritize departures for incoming arrivals above all else. Held-inventory departures are deferred to the next day.

## Documentation

- **Daily Assignment Sheet:** Shared document in HotSOS and paper backup at the Housekeeping office.
- **Room Status log in OPERA Cloud:** Source of truth for real-time status.
- **Checkpoint notes:** Brief entries in the Housekeeping shift log at each checkpoint.
- **Shift end recap:** Director logs the day's stayover service rate (rooms serviced / rooms assigned) and departure completion time.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the General Manager if:
- A pre-assigned early arrival room cannot be ready by the promised time
- Staffing shortfall will prevent all 3:00 PM arrivals from being housed on time
- A repeated pattern of missed stayovers emerges over multiple days
