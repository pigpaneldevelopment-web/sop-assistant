# Daily Rate Review and Yield Decision Procedure

**Property:** The Pelican House, Destin, FL
**Department:** Revenue Management
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

The daily rate review is where the property's pricing discipline lives or dies. The Pelican House uses IDeaS G3 for automated pricing recommendations, but the system does not override judgment — it informs it. This procedure defines how the General Manager (who holds the Revenue Manager function at this property) conducts the daily rate review, when to accept the system's recommendation, when to override it, and how every yield decision is documented. Because Gulf Coast beach demand is highly seasonal and event-driven, rates require more active management here than at a steady urban property.

## Scope

Applies to the General Manager. Covers the daily rate review for all room types across a 90-day forward window, with extended review on peak dates, holidays, and known event periods (Destin Fishing Rodeo, spring break weeks, major holiday weekends).

## Responsibilities

- **General Manager / Revenue Manager:** Performs the daily rate review; makes the yield decision; publishes rates across all channels.
- **Front Desk Agent:** Flags any rate discrepancy observed during guest bookings to the GM the same day.
- **Ownership:** Reviews the month-end pace and yield report; does not participate in daily pricing.

## Timing

The rate review is performed every business day, Monday through Friday. Weekends are handled via a Sunday evening review that covers Sunday, Monday tactical updates, and any pre-planned promotional changes. Rate decisions implemented before 10:00 AM capture a full day of OTA search visibility; decisions made later still apply but with reduced effect.

## Tools

- **IDeaS G3:** Automated pricing recommendation engine; pulls demand signals, comp set rates, and booking pace.
- **Mews:** Property PMS; source of occupancy, pickup, and pace data.
- **STR / Kalibri reports:** Comp set performance data, reviewed weekly on Tuesdays.
- **Rate Shop Report:** Weekly competitive rate scan; see the Competitive Rate Shop Procedure SOP.
- **OTA Extranets:** Expedia, Booking.com, and the direct booking engine.

## The Daily Rate Review Sequence

### Step 1 — Read the Day (15 minutes)

1. Open Mews. Note yesterday's actual pickup: how many rooms sold, which room types, at what average rate.
2. Open IDeaS G3. Review the "What Changed" section since the last review. The system summarizes demand shifts it has detected.
3. Review the 7-day forward pace against prior year. Pace ahead of prior year suggests demand is stronger; pace behind suggests it is weakening.
4. Review the 30-day and 60-day forward pace. These windows catch the slower-building demand curves for longer stays.
5. Review any events, weather forecasts, or news that affect demand for the windows being priced.

### Step 2 — Review IDeaS Recommendations (20 minutes)

IDeaS G3 produces rate recommendations daily across the forward window. The GM's job is to evaluate each recommendation, not rubber-stamp it.

1. Open the IDeaS G3 Rate Recommendations screen.
2. Filter to the next 30 days.
3. For each day, review the recommended rate, the occupancy forecast, and the reasoning the system provides.
4. Accept recommendations where the logic is sound and the GM has no contradicting property-level information.
5. Override where the GM has information the system does not have. Common override cases:
   - Upcoming event the system does not know about (local festival, wedding at a nearby venue)
   - Unusual weather forecast (tropical storm, unseasonable cold snap)
   - Group block not fully captured in the system's view
   - Competitive positioning decision (e.g., "We're not leading Destin on this weekend — hold at second-from-top")
6. Document any override in the IDeaS Decision Notes with the specific reason. This creates an audit trail and teaches the system over time.

### Step 3 — Review the Rate Shop (10 minutes, Tuesdays primary)

The competitive rate shop runs weekly (see Competitive Rate Shop Procedure SOP). On Tuesdays, integrate the fresh data:

1. Compare The Pelican House's current rates against the comp set for the next 30 days.
2. Identify any date where the property is materially above or below the comp set mid-point.
3. For dates where we are well above: confirm demand supports it; if not, compress toward the mid-point.
4. For dates where we are well below: confirm the discounted position is intentional (e.g., responding to a slow forecast); if not, raise.

### Step 4 — Review Booking Pace and Demand Signals (10 minutes)

1. Run the 90-Day Pace Report in Mews.
2. Identify dates where pace is significantly ahead or behind prior year.
3. Ahead-of-pace dates are candidates for rate increase — don't leave money on the table.
4. Behind-pace dates are candidates for rate adjustment, promotion, or channel-specific action.
5. For weekends 30 days out that are behind pace, consider enabling an advance-purchase promotion or a bounce-back offer for recent guests.

### Step 5 — Make the Yield Decisions (10 minutes)

For each date reviewed, the GM lands on one of four decisions:

- **Hold:** Current rate is right. No action.
- **Raise:** Demand supports a price increase. Specify the new rate and effective time.
- **Lower:** Demand is not materializing at current rate. Specify the new rate and effective time.
- **Promote:** Rate stays at BAR but a promotion (advance purchase, length-of-stay discount, package) is activated.

Document each decision in the Yield Log spreadsheet in the shared drive. Fields: date of review, target date, decision, from rate, to rate, reasoning.

### Step 6 — Publish Rates (10 minutes)

1. In IDeaS G3, accept or override the rates per the decisions made.
2. IDeaS pushes to Mews automatically. Verify the push completed on the Channel Status screen.
3. Spot-check by searching as a guest on the property website and on Expedia for 2–3 of the changed dates. Confirm rates match.
4. If rates do not match, troubleshoot: check Mews for the updated rate, check the channel manager push, manually push if the automated job failed.

## Overbooking Context

Rate decisions connect directly to overbooking decisions at high-occupancy periods. When the GM is deliberately overbooking a date (standard practice at this property on historically high-attrition dates), rates are set accordingly — typically held at BAR rather than discounted. See the Overbooking Management and Walk Procedure SOP.

## Peak Date and Event Override

For known peak dates (Memorial Day weekend, July 4th, Destin Fishing Rodeo, Labor Day weekend, spring break windows, Thanksgiving, Christmas, New Year):

1. Rate review is conducted 120 days out, 90 days out, 60 days out, 30 days out, 14 days out, and 7 days out — not just daily.
2. Minimum length-of-stay restrictions are evaluated at each checkpoint.
3. IDeaS recommendations are treated as a floor at peak dates; the GM actively looks for opportunities to push beyond.

## Documentation

- **Yield Log:** Daily spreadsheet entry in the shared drive; every decision logged with reasoning.
- **IDeaS Decision Notes:** Override reasoning captured in the system itself.
- **Monthly Yield Report:** Generated by the GM at month-end; reviewed with ownership.

## Common Mistakes

- Accepting all IDeaS recommendations without reading them — the system is a tool, not a replacement for judgment.
- Overriding IDeaS without documenting why — the system learns from documented feedback; undocumented overrides are noise.
- Reviewing only the next 7 days — 30-day and 60-day windows are where pace problems need to be caught.
