# Allergy and Dietary Restriction Handling

**Property:** Juniper Creek Ranch, Fredericksburg, TX
**Department:** Food & Beverage
**Document Owner:** F&B Supervisor
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Allergy and dietary restriction handling is a non-negotiable safety procedure at Juniper Creek Ranch. Guests who inform us of an allergy are placing their trust in our process. This procedure defines how every dietary communication is captured, relayed, and confirmed across the Front Office, the kitchen, and the service team — from the reservation until the plate is delivered. A missed step here is not a service failure; it is a safety failure.

## Scope

Applies to all F&B Servers, Bartenders, F&B Supervisors, and line cooks. Also applies to Front Desk Agents and Reservations staff who capture dietary information at booking or check-in. Covers restaurant dining, private events, group dining, and in-room dining.

## Responsibilities

- **Front Desk Agent / Reservations:** Captures dietary information in the Maestro Guest Profile at booking or check-in; repeats information back to the guest to confirm.
- **Server:** Confirms dietary information verbally with the guest before ordering; documents every allergy on the Toast ticket; verifies dishes at pickup.
- **F&B Supervisor:** Verifies allergy flow from ticket to plate; personally delivers every allergy-flagged dish or supervises delivery.
- **Line Cook:** Prepares allergy-flagged dishes on a separated station with clean tools and surfaces; confirms plate to F&B Supervisor before it leaves the line.
- **General Manager:** Reviews any allergy incident within 24 hours; owns communication to ownership or legal counsel if escalated.

## Terminology

Precise language matters in this domain. Use the following consistently across all team communication.

- **Allergy:** A medical reaction to a specific food, including severe reactions (anaphylaxis). Flagged as "ALLERGY" on all documentation.
- **Intolerance:** A non-allergic adverse reaction (e.g., lactose intolerance). Flagged as "INTOLERANCE" on documentation.
- **Preference:** A dietary choice (vegetarian, vegan, low-sodium). Flagged as "PREFERENCE."

Do not combine or blur these categories. An allergy and a preference require different handling.

## Capturing Information at the Front End

### At Reservation

When a guest books and volunteers dietary information:

1. Record in the Maestro Guest Profile under the Dietary Notes field.
2. Use the exact terminology from above — Allergy, Intolerance, or Preference.
3. Specify the item: "ALLERGY — shellfish." Not "seafood issues."
4. Repeat back: "So I have noted that you have a shellfish allergy — I'll make sure the restaurant team is aware on every visit during your stay."
5. If the reservation is via an OTA or phone and the guest reports a severe allergy, flag the reservation for the F&B Supervisor to call the guest before arrival.

### At Check-In

Front Desk Agent confirms dietary notes verbally with every arriving guest:

1. Review the Guest Profile in Maestro before the guest arrives.
2. At check-in, acknowledge: "I see we have a note on a shellfish allergy — is that still current?"
3. Update the profile if anything has changed.
4. Send the updated notes to the F&B Supervisor's daily briefing email at the end of each check-in shift.

## At the Table

### Order Taking

1. Before taking any order, the Server confirms with every guest: "Are there any allergies or dietary restrictions I should share with the kitchen?"
2. Do not skip this step even if the Guest Profile shows no notes. A guest may have developed an allergy since their last stay or may have a guest at the table with their own needs.
3. If an allergy is reported, enter it on the Toast ticket using the "ALLERGY — [item]" modifier on every course. The modifier must appear on every course — appetizer, entree, dessert, sides. Not just the entree.
4. Verbally confirm back to the guest: "Got it — we'll handle the shellfish allergy across the full meal."
5. Ask if cross-contact is a concern. Some guests tolerate shared cooking surfaces; others require fully segregated prep.
6. Record the cross-contact answer in the Toast order notes.

### Kitchen Flow

1. Toast routes all allergy-flagged tickets to the expo printer with a red highlight strip.
2. The line cook pulls a clean cutting board, clean pan, and fresh gloves before starting any allergy-flagged dish.
3. Prep is performed on the designated allergy station at the end of the hot line. The station is wiped, sanitized, and re-set before each allergy-flagged order.
4. The dish is plated separately from other dishes in the party. No garnish is added that wasn't explicitly listed in the ticket.
5. The line cook calls "allergy dish up" when the plate is ready. The F&B Supervisor responds in person — not by radio — before the dish is picked up.

### Delivery

1. The F&B Supervisor personally delivers the allergy-flagged dish, or supervises the Server doing so.
2. At the table, confirm with the guest: "This is the [dish] prepared for the shellfish allergy." Wait for guest acknowledgment before placing the plate.
3. If any doubt exists about cross-contact, the plate is returned to the kitchen and remade.

## Severe Allergy / Anaphylaxis Risk

If a guest reports a severe allergy (anaphylaxis risk):

1. Server notifies the F&B Supervisor immediately — before taking the order.
2. F&B Supervisor personally meets the guest at the table to confirm details.
3. Kitchen reviews menu items and confirms what can be safely prepared. If the kitchen cannot guarantee safety for a specific dish, say so directly to the guest. Do not guess.
4. Offer modifications only where the kitchen has confirmed they are safe. Do not improvise.
5. F&B Supervisor personally delivers every course.

## If a Reaction Occurs

1. Stop service at the table immediately.
2. Ask the guest if they have an EpiPen and whether they need assistance. If yes, assist and call 911.
3. If the guest loses consciousness, call 911 without hesitation.
4. Notify the Manager on Duty and General Manager immediately. The GM escalates to ownership.
5. Do not discard the dish, the ticket, or any related prep. These are needed for the incident investigation.
6. Complete a Guest Incident Report within the shift. Kitchen retains the ticket and any plating notes.
7. Do not discuss fault or cause with the guest or their party. Any statement is made only by the General Manager.

## Documentation

- Every allergy flag is logged in Maestro Guest Profile and carried forward to future stays.
- Toast retains the ticket for 90 days.
- Any incident generates a Guest Incident Report filed in the GM's office within 24 hours.

## Common Failure Modes

- Allergy noted on the entree ticket but not the appetizer — cross-contact in a shared starter is the most common real-world incident.
- Server assumes the kitchen "knows" because the guest is a return visitor — verify every time.
- Substitution made at the line without cook verification — modifications get made silently under pressure.
