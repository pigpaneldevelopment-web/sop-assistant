# Walk-In Reservation Handling

**Property:** The Ironwood, Sedona, AZ
**Department:** Front Office
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Walk-ins at The Ironwood fall into two categories: the couple on a Sedona road trip who didn't plan ahead, and the displaced traveler whose previous hotel fell through. Both are high-value bookings — walk-ins typically pay closer to BAR than any other channel and produce strong guest loyalty when handled well. This procedure defines how a walk-in is qualified, quoted, sold, and converted at The Ironwood, and when to decline.

## Scope

Applies to all Front Desk Agents and Night Auditors. Covers walk-ins during normal desk hours and overnight. Does not cover pre-booked reservations arriving the same day — those are handled under the Arrival and Check-In Procedure SOP.

## Responsibilities

- **Front Desk Agent:** Qualifies the walk-in, quotes the rate, converts the sale, and processes the reservation in OPERA Cloud.
- **Night Auditor:** Handles walk-ins between 11:00 PM and 7:00 AM using the same procedure, with added ID verification per overnight security standards.
- **Manager on Duty:** Approves any walk-in rate deviation below the posted floor; handles any walk-in turn-away for cause.
- **General Manager:** Reviews walk-in conversion rates monthly as part of pickup analysis.

## Rate Strategy

The Ironwood does not deeply discount walk-ins. Guests who arrive unannounced at a destination property have limited alternatives — pricing should reflect that. The walk-in floor is set daily by the GM based on forecast occupancy.

- **High-occupancy nights (80%+):** Walk-in rate = BAR. No discretion.
- **Mid-occupancy nights (60–79%):** Walk-in rate = BAR; FDA may offer up to 10% off for an on-the-spot close.
- **Low-occupancy nights (under 60%):** Walk-in rate = BAR; FDA may offer up to 15% off. Below 15% requires Manager approval.

The walk-in floor is posted at the front desk each morning. Do not improvise below the floor without Manager authorization.

## Qualification Sequence

Not every walk-in is a good match for The Ironwood. Qualifying first protects both the guest and the property.

1. Greet the guest warmly: "Welcome to The Ironwood — looking for a room tonight?"
2. Ask how many nights and how many guests. Confirm arrival is tonight (not a future date — those are reservations, not walk-ins).
3. Ask the purpose of the stay if it surfaces naturally. Never interrogate, but a context clue shapes the recommendation — a couple for an anniversary wants different than a solo business traveler.
4. Check OPERA Cloud for availability. If multiple room types are open, identify two options before quoting.

## Quoting Sequence

1. Never lead with price. Lead with the product.
2. Describe two options: a standard option (usually the lowest-tier available) and an upgraded option (suite, view, or amenity-differentiated). Example: "We have our Desert King at $319 — that's our standard king with a small patio — or we have the Red Rock Suite at $429, which has a private balcony facing the rocks, a soaking tub, and more space."
3. Pause. Let the guest respond. Most walk-ins will pick the upgraded option when given the chance.
4. If they ask for the lowest rate without prompting, quote the standard option. Do not volunteer a discount.
5. If the guest hesitates at the rate, present the value rather than discount: "That rate includes our morning breakfast and access to the spa's outdoor pool — no resort fee." If they still hesitate, then discount per the rate strategy above.

## Closing the Sale

1. Once the guest accepts, move to the reservation screen in OPERA Cloud.
2. Collect: full name, phone number, email, photo ID, payment method.
3. Scan the ID into the Guest Card.
4. Process full payment up front for walk-ins. The Ironwood's policy is pre-payment at booking for walk-ins, not authorization-only.
5. If the guest prefers to pay in cash, collect the full stay total plus a $150 incidental cash deposit per night. Issue a cash receipt per the Cash Handling SOP.
6. Assign a room. Walk-ins go to inspected rooms only — no "will be ready" commitments unless the guest is willing to wait in the lobby with a defined wait time.
7. Complete the standard check-in per the Arrival and Check-In Procedure SOP.

## After-Hours Walk-Ins

Walk-ins between 11:00 PM and 7:00 AM carry elevated scrutiny because most legitimate travelers arrive earlier or have reservations.

1. Greet the guest and complete qualification per the standard procedure.
2. Verify photo ID before any rate is quoted. If the ID is expired, unverifiable, or appears tampered with, politely decline and suggest alternate options in town.
3. Do not accept cash from after-hours walk-ins without the Night Auditor's sign-off and a photograph of the ID attached to the cash receipt.
4. If anything feels off — the guest is evasive about their purpose, appears impaired, is asking questions about other guests, or is accompanied by a third party who remains outside — pause the reservation and step into the back office to contact the Manager on Duty by cell before proceeding.
5. Document any declined walk-in in the Shift Notes with the reason. Never document in a way that could be perceived as discriminatory — facts only, no assumptions.

## Declining a Walk-In

The Ironwood reserves the right to decline a walk-in reservation. Legitimate grounds for declining:

- The guest cannot provide valid government-issued photo ID.
- The guest cannot provide acceptable payment (declined card and insufficient cash deposit).
- The guest appears impaired to a degree that poses safety risk.
- The guest is on the property's Do Not Rent list.
- The guest is displaying behavior that would create a disturbance for other guests.

When declining:

1. Do so politely and without elaboration. "I'm sorry, I'm not able to book you a room tonight."
2. Do not argue or explain in detail. Long explanations invite escalation.
3. If the guest asks for a reason and it is safe to provide one, give only the most minimal factual reason ("we require a verifiable ID").
4. Offer the number for the town's visitor center or a nearby property if safe to do so.
5. If the guest becomes aggressive, contact the Manager on Duty and, if necessary, 911.
6. Document the incident in the Shift Notes and file a Guest Incident Report. Any Do Not Rent decision requires Manager approval.

## Walk-In Conversion Tracking

Walk-in inquiries that do not book are as important as those that do. Every walk-in conversation is logged:

1. In the Walk-In Log (shared document at the desk), note the time, party size, nights requested, and result (booked / declined by guest / declined by property).
2. For declined-by-guest entries, note the reason if the guest volunteered one (price, location, amenity). This data informs rate and product decisions.
3. The GM reviews the Walk-In Log weekly.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the Manager on Duty if:
- The guest requests a rate below the day's floor
- The guest appears impaired, evasive, or aggressive
- The guest's ID cannot be verified
- The property is within 5 rooms of sellout and walk-in strategy needs review
- Any walk-in requests accommodation that raises an ADA or accessibility question (see the ADA Service Animal Policy SOP and related accessibility SOPs)
