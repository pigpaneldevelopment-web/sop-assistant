# No-Show Processing and Charge Policy

**Property:** Juniper Creek Ranch, Fredericksburg, TX
**Department:** Night Audit
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

A no-show is a reservation that does not arrive and is not cancelled within the cancellation window. Juniper Creek Ranch's no-show policy protects revenue against guests who don't show without cancelling, while handling legitimate exceptions (flight delay, family emergency, weather) with judgment. This procedure defines how no-shows are identified, charged, documented, and disputed.

## Scope

Applies to the Night Auditor, Front Desk Agents handling next-day disputes, and the General Manager who adjudicates exceptions. Covers direct bookings, OTA bookings (Expedia, Booking.com), and group reservations. Group no-shows follow the Group Billing and Master Folio Management SOP for reconciliation.

## Responsibilities

- **Front Desk Agent (evening):** Confirms each pending arrival against the Arrivals Report; initiates late-arrival outreach for guests not yet in-house by 10:00 PM.
- **Night Auditor:** Executes the no-show processing sequence before the 3:00 AM audit; posts the no-show charges; documents exceptions.
- **General Manager:** Reviews all no-show charges as part of the morning operations check-in; adjudicates disputes.
- **Revenue Manager (GM functions):** Reviews no-show patterns monthly; adjusts channel-specific policy if needed.

## Policy

The no-show charge at Juniper Creek Ranch equals one night's room and tax, charged to the payment method on file. This policy applies unless superseded by an OTA-specific policy (some OTAs enforce their own cancellation windows and penalty rules).

- **Direct bookings:** Cancel by 4:00 PM local time on arrival day to avoid charge.
- **Expedia:** Expedia's cancellation policy governs, typically 24 hours before arrival unless the property sets otherwise.
- **Booking.com:** Booking.com's cancellation policy governs, with the property's no-show window applied to whatever the guest selected at booking.
- **Groups:** Per contract, typically 72 hours before arrival per reservation.
- **Advance-purchase rates:** Non-refundable at booking; no no-show charge applies because the full stay has already been captured.

## Pre-Audit Sequence (Evening Shift)

The evening Front Desk Agent prepares the no-show handoff before going off shift.

### 6:00 PM Check

1. Open the Arrivals Report in Maestro for today.
2. Identify any expected arrival marked as "Pending Arrival" that has not yet checked in.
3. For each pending arrival, review the Guest Card for late-arrival notes, confirmed flight delays, or prior communication.
4. Initiate an SMS or email outreach to arrivals expected before 6:00 PM who have not arrived: "We wanted to check in — do you have an updated arrival time? We'll have the desk staffed for your late arrival."

### 10:00 PM Check

1. Review the Arrivals Report again.
2. Any guest not arrived and not communicated with is flagged "No Contact" in the Guest Card notes.
3. Pass the list of pending arrivals to the Night Auditor at shift handover per the Shift Handover SOP.

## No-Show Processing Sequence (Overnight)

The Night Auditor executes the following before the 3:00 AM audit runs.

### Step 1 — Identify

1. At 12:00 AM, open the Arrivals Report filtered to "Pending Arrival."
2. Any reservation still showing pending at this hour is a no-show candidate.
3. Review each candidate's Guest Card for any late-arrival communication or exception note.

### Step 2 — Evaluate

For each candidate, determine the category:

- **Standard no-show:** No communication, no exception. Proceed to charge.
- **Late-arrival communication in Guest Card:** Guest has confirmed they will arrive after midnight. Do not charge. Leave reservation open; handle at arrival per After-Hours Check-In Procedure SOP.
- **Cancellation received but not processed:** Occasionally a guest cancels through an OTA and the cancellation has not yet synced. Check the OTA extranet for Expedia and Booking.com reservations before charging.
- **Flight delay or travel disruption:** If the Guest Card notes or email threads reference a documented disruption, do not charge. Mark the reservation "No-Show — Exception" and pass to the GM for review.

### Step 3 — Charge

For reservations categorized as standard no-show:

1. Open the reservation in Maestro.
2. Mark the reservation "No-Show" status. This action triggers the no-show charge posting automatically.
3. The system posts one night's room and tax to the reservation folio.
4. Payment is automatically processed against the card on file.
5. If the card declines, do not re-attempt immediately. Flag the reservation in the Collections Queue and hand to the GM in the morning.

### Step 4 — Document

For every no-show processed, document in the No-Show Log (shared spreadsheet):

- Date of no-show
- Reservation number
- Guest name
- Source (direct, Expedia, Booking.com, etc.)
- Charge amount
- Payment status (posted, declined, flagged for follow-up)
- Exception noted (if any)

The No-Show Log is reviewed by the GM every morning.

### Step 5 — Release the Room

1. After the no-show charge is posted, the room is released in Maestro and becomes available for walk-in or next-day transient sale.
2. If the no-show room had been assigned a specific room number (pre-blocked), un-assign the room to return it to the Rooms Forecast.

## Exception Handling

The following scenarios require judgment and documentation rather than a reflexive charge.

### Documented Flight Delay

If a guest has emailed, called, or messaged documenting a flight delay or cancellation:

1. Do not charge.
2. Mark reservation "No-Show — Exception — Travel Disruption."
3. Document the guest's communication in the Guest Card.
4. Hold the reservation open for 24 hours. Reach out the next day to rebook or confirm cancellation.

### Medical or Family Emergency

If the guest or a family member contacts the property citing a medical or family emergency:

1. Do not charge.
2. Mark reservation "No-Show — Exception — Emergency."
3. The GM reviews the next morning. Standard handling is waiver of the charge and offer to rebook.
4. Do not request documentation of the emergency. Take the guest at their word.

### Weather Event

If a regional weather event (ice storm, hurricane, major highway closure) is documented for the guest's origin or arrival route:

1. Review the event in the local news or via weather services.
2. Do not charge for no-shows originating from the affected region on the event day.
3. Mark "No-Show — Exception — Weather."
4. Proactively waive charges for a defined geographic radius at the GM's direction.

### OTA Pending Cancellation

If the OTA extranet shows the reservation was cancelled but the cancellation has not yet synced to Maestro:

1. Do not charge. The cancellation is valid.
2. Mark the reservation "Cancelled" manually in Maestro.
3. Document the OTA cancellation confirmation number in the Guest Card.

## Guest Disputes

If a guest disputes a no-show charge after the fact:

1. The FDA or GM receiving the dispute opens the reservation in Maestro.
2. Review the Arrivals Report from the day in question, the Guest Card notes, and any communication on file.
3. If the charge is clearly correct, explain politely and cite the cancellation policy from the reservation confirmation.
4. If the guest's story has any support (they claim a family emergency, they claim they called to cancel), the GM reviews and generally extends the benefit of the doubt for a first-time dispute.
5. Document the dispute and resolution in the Guest Card.
6. Refund processing, if approved, is handled by the GM through Maestro.

## Documentation

- **No-Show Log:** Updated daily. Reviewed every morning by the GM.
- **Guest Card notes:** Every exception, communication, and dispute documented at the reservation level.
- **Monthly No-Show Report:** Compiled by the GM; reviewed for patterns (specific OTAs with elevated rates, specific dates, etc.).

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the Manager on Duty or General Manager if:
- A guest disputes a no-show charge during the overnight shift
- A no-show charge declines and the amount exceeds $500
- A pattern of suspicious no-shows from the same guest or card emerges
- An exception category does not fit clearly into the defined categories
