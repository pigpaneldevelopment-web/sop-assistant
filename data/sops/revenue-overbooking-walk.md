# Overbooking Management and Walk Procedure

**Property:** The Cannon & Vine, Nashville, TN
**Department:** Revenue Management
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Overbooking is a deliberate revenue management tool, not an accident. When done well, it protects the property against cancellations and no-shows and keeps rooms from sitting empty on sold-out nights. When done poorly, it forces "walks" — relocating a confirmed guest to another property — that damage trust and carry hard and soft costs. This procedure defines how The Cannon & Vine forecasts, approves, monitors, and executes overbookings, and when and how to walk a guest if the math didn't work.

## Scope

Applies to the General Manager (who holds the Revenue Manager function), Front Desk Agents, and Night Auditor. Covers daily overbooking decisions, peak-period overbooking strategy, and walk execution when the house cannot absorb the overbook. Group overbooking is coordinated with the group's contract terms and is not covered here.

## Responsibilities

- **General Manager:** Makes all overbooking decisions; approves the walk decision; negotiates with sister hotels for walk placements; handles any guest escalation.
- **Front Desk Agent:** Monitors the Arrivals vs. Availability screen throughout the shift; escalates any emerging overbook situation to the GM; executes the walk if directed.
- **Night Auditor:** Confirms final no-show count before the 3:00 AM audit; adjusts overbook status based on actual arrivals.

## Why Overbook

On a typical night, a percentage of confirmed reservations do not arrive — some cancel within the window, some no-show, some cancel through an OTA that does not sync instantly. Historical data shows cancellation and no-show rates of 3–6% for direct bookings and 8–12% for OTA bookings. Selling to 100% of availability means the property sells to 94–97% occupancy in practice. Controlled overbooking closes that gap.

## The Overbooking Tolerance

The GM sets an overbooking tolerance each day based on:

- Historical cancellation/no-show rate for the day of week
- Current forecast occupancy
- Booking pace
- Known factors (weather, regional events, competing property status)

Typical tolerances at The Cannon & Vine (75-room property):

- **Low season weeknights:** Overbook up to 3 rooms.
- **Mid-week standard:** Overbook up to 2 rooms.
- **Peak weekends (no events):** Overbook up to 2 rooms.
- **Peak weekends with event compression (CMA Fest, major concerts):** Overbook 0–1 rooms. High demand also means no walk inventory at competing properties.
- **Christmas/New Year:** Overbook 0 rooms. Sleep at night.

The tolerance is a ceiling, not a target. The GM reviews the tolerance daily and adjusts based on actual pace.

## Setting the Daily Overbook

Each morning, the GM updates the overbook ceiling in Cloudbeds for the 14-day forward window.

1. Open the Rooms Availability screen in Cloudbeds.
2. Review each date's forecast occupancy and booking pace.
3. For dates projected to sell out, set the overbook ceiling per the tolerance above.
4. Document the overbook decision for peak dates in the Yield Log.
5. Confirm the channel manager is respecting the ceiling — overbook limits push to Expedia, Booking.com, and the direct booking engine automatically.

## Daily Overbook Monitoring

The Front Desk Agent and GM monitor the overbook status throughout the arrival day.

### Morning (9:00 AM)

- Run the Arrivals Report for the day and night.
- Count confirmed arrivals vs. available inventory.
- If the gap is within forecast cancellation/no-show range, hold the course.
- If the gap is wider than expected, flag for early intervention.

### Afternoon (2:00 PM)

- Review cancellations received since morning.
- Review pace on OTA extranets for any late same-day bookings that increased the gap.
- If the overbook is resolving (cancellations coming in), proceed.
- If not, begin walk preparation before the first affected arrival.

### Evening (6:00 PM)

- Count in-house vs. expected remaining arrivals.
- Identify the rooms remaining and whether every remaining arrival can be housed.
- If there is a shortage, the GM makes the walk decision by 6:30 PM — before the majority of late arrivals.

## Walk Preparation

Walking a guest is executed with a specific sequence designed to minimize guest harm and preserve the relationship.

### Who Gets Walked

1. Walk the most recent booking first — OTAs and direct.
2. Walk one-night stays before multi-night stays when possible.
3. Never walk VIPs, return guests, guests on special occasions, or guests paying for a specific room type that cannot be replicated elsewhere.
4. Do not walk a guest who has already checked in — if the property is full and a reservation discrepancy emerges, find another solution.

### Where They Get Walked

The GM maintains a walk-partner list — three comparable properties in Nashville with reciprocal walk relationships.

1. Call the first property on the list. Request a room for the night at an equivalent room type.
2. If confirmed, the property (The Cannon & Vine) pays the rate at the partner property, not the guest. The guest is never out-of-pocket on a walk.
3. If the first partner is full, move to the next. The GM calls directly — this is not delegated to the FDA for a first-time walk handling.
4. Confirm the partner property's address, check-in instructions, and rate before calling the guest.

## Executing the Walk

When the guest arrives or calls with an expected arrival time, the GM handles the conversation in person where possible.

### The Conversation

1. Greet the guest warmly as normal.
2. Acknowledge the situation directly: "I owe you an apology. We've had a situation tonight where we're oversold and we're not going to be able to honor your reservation here this evening."
3. Do not elaborate on reasons. Do not blame a third party. Do not say "I don't know how this happened."
4. Present the arrangement: "What I've done is arranged a room for you at [partner property] at no cost to you for tonight. We'll cover your room there. I have transportation ready for you now."
5. Offer the resolution package:
   - Full comp of tonight's room at the partner property (property pays).
   - Return transportation to The Cannon & Vine tomorrow if the guest is multi-night.
   - One night of the remaining stay comped at The Cannon & Vine (if multi-night, the guest gets one comp'd night from the remaining stay).
   - Upgrade on return (if inventory allows).
   - A sincere apology, in writing, signed by the GM, delivered to the guest.
6. Confirm the guest's contact details for the next day. Confirm the partner property's address and driver's information.
7. Answer any question the guest has honestly. Do not overpromise.
8. Escort the guest to the transportation.

### What Not to Do

- Do not describe the walk as "upgrading" the guest or frame it as a benefit.
- Do not ask the guest to drive themselves unless they insist.
- Do not assign this conversation to a Front Desk Agent. The GM owns this.
- Do not walk without a confirmed partner room.

## Next-Day Follow-Up

The GM personally contacts the walked guest the next morning.

1. Call or email by 10:00 AM.
2. Confirm the walk went smoothly.
3. Confirm transportation is arranged if the guest is returning.
4. Reiterate the resolution.
5. Ask if there is anything else the guest needs.

## Documentation

- **Yield Log:** Daily overbook decision recorded.
- **Walk Log:** Every walk documented with date, guest name, reservation number, reason, partner property, cost, and resolution package.
- **Guest Card:** Detailed notes added to the walked guest's profile so any future stay reflects the incident and the property team can recognize the guest on return.
- **Daily Operations Meeting:** Any walk from the prior night is discussed at the morning meeting — what the signal was, why it didn't resolve, what to do differently.

## Monthly Review

The GM reviews overbook and walk performance monthly.

1. Total overbook decisions vs. actual walks executed.
2. Walk-to-overbook ratio — walks should be rare relative to overbook decisions.
3. Any pattern (specific days of week, specific channels driving cancellations) that warrants tolerance adjustment.
4. Partner property reciprocity balance — if the property has been walking more guests to partners than receiving, reassess the tolerance.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact ownership if:
- The number of walks in a month exceeds 5
- A walked guest demands compensation above the standard resolution package
- A walked guest refuses the walk and insists on being housed at the property
- A partner property declines to reciprocate going forward
- Any media or review incident arises from a walk
