# Linen and Towel Exchange Program

**Property:** The Pelican House, Destin, FL
**Department:** Housekeeping
**Document Owner:** Director of Housekeeping
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

The Pelican House's linen and towel exchange program is the backbone of both operational efficiency and our sustainability commitment. A structured program protects linen life, reduces laundry costs, lowers water and energy use, and gives guests the choice to participate — without compromising the experience of those who want daily refreshes. This procedure defines how the program is executed in stayover rooms, how linens are tracked and rotated, and how the team handles exceptions.

## Scope

Applies to all Room Attendants, Inspectors, and the Laundry Team. Covers stayover room service only — departure cleans always receive a full linen and towel strip per the Room Cleaning Sequence SOP. Covers bed linens, bath towels, pool towels (tracked separately), and beach towels (seasonal inventory).

## Responsibilities

- **Room Attendant:** Applies the exchange rules below on every stayover clean; documents any replacement in Mews.
- **Inspector:** Verifies the exchange was handled correctly during spot inspections.
- **Director of Housekeeping:** Maintains par levels; orders replacement linens; audits program compliance monthly.
- **Laundry Team:** Tracks soiled linen counts against room attendant logs; flags discrepancies.

## The Program at a Glance

Guests at The Pelican House receive a card in their room explaining the linen exchange program. The policy reads: sheets changed every 3 nights by default; towels replaced when left on the floor or in the tub; guests may opt out of linen changes or request a daily change at any time.

## Sheet Exchange Rules

The Room Attendant applies the following rules on every stayover clean.

### Default: 3-Night Change

1. Check the Guest Card in Mews for the guest's arrival date.
2. Calculate nights stayed as of today's clean.
3. On nights 1 and 2, make the bed with existing linens (the bed is still made neatly, just not stripped).
4. On night 3, strip the bed and replace with fresh linens. Update the Guest Card note with "Linens changed [date]."
5. Continue the 3-night cadence: next change on night 6, then 9, and so on.

### Guest-Requested Daily Change

If the guest has requested daily linen changes (noted in the Guest Card or indicated by the in-room change-today card):

1. Strip and replace linens every stayover clean.
2. Do not apply the 3-night rule regardless of stay length.

### Guest-Requested Opt-Out

If the guest has requested no linen changes for the stay:

1. Do not strip or replace linens unless they are visibly soiled.
2. Remake the bed each day with existing linens.
3. Note "No linen change — guest request" in the Guest Card.

### Mandatory Change Conditions

Regardless of the schedule above, linens are stripped and replaced immediately if any of the following are present:

- Visible soiling (blood, bodily fluid, food, makeup, dirt)
- Strong odor
- Evidence of illness (vomit, used tissues in bed)
- Any tear, pull, or staining that affects presentation

Do not attempt to spot-clean linen in the room. Strip and replace.

## Towel Exchange Rules

Towel exchange follows the "on the floor or in the tub" standard common to hospitality sustainability programs.

1. Bath towels left hanging on hooks or bars are considered reusable by the guest. Do not replace.
2. Bath towels on the floor or draped over the tub are replaced with fresh towels.
3. Hand towels are replaced daily by default — they accumulate heavier use per surface area.
4. Face cloths are replaced daily by default.
5. Bathmats are replaced daily by default.
6. Pool towels and beach towels are tracked separately (see below) and handled outside this rule.

### Common Mistakes

- Replacing all towels regardless of placement — defeats the program and adds laundry cost.
- Leaving a lightly used towel on a hook when the guest has clearly moved it to the floor — misses the guest's signal.
- Skipping hand towel or face cloth replacement because bath towels were left on hooks — these are on a daily cadence regardless of the bath towel.

## Pool and Beach Towel Tracking

Because The Pelican House sits on the Gulf, pool and beach towels are a significant inventory and cost. They are tracked separately from room towels.

### Pool Towels

1. Pool towels are issued at the pool attendant's stand. Guests sign a towel log with their name and room number.
2. Each guest receives up to 4 pool towels per day without charge.
3. At the end of the day, pool towels are collected at the pool towel return bin.
4. The pool attendant reconciles the issued count against returned count at shift end. A variance over 10% is reported to the Director of Housekeeping.

### Beach Towels

1. Beach towels are delivered to guest rooms on request and recorded in the Guest Card.
2. Beach towels are oversized and are tracked by serial number stitched into the hem.
3. On departure, any beach towel not returned is charged to the guest at $45 per towel.
4. The FDA verifies beach towel return status before closing the folio at check-out.

## Linen Rotation and Par

The Director of Housekeeping maintains par as follows:

- **Bed linens:** 3.5 sets per room (one on the bed, one in the laundry cycle, one on the shelf, plus half-par for peak).
- **Bath towels:** 4 sets per room.
- **Hand towels:** 6 per room.
- **Face cloths:** 4 per room.
- **Bathmats:** 3 per room.
- **Pool towels:** 300 rotating across pool inventory.
- **Beach towels:** 150 serialized, with loss target under 5%.

Par is audited monthly. Replacement orders are placed when any category drops below 80% of full par.

## Rag-Out Standards

Linens and towels exit the rotation when:

- Visible staining cannot be removed after two laundering attempts.
- Tears, pulls, or fraying affect presentation.
- Color has faded beyond the acceptable threshold set by the Director of Housekeeping.

Rag-out linens are diverted to rag-room use (in-house cleaning cloths) or donated to local animal shelters. They do not return to guest room circulation.

## Documentation

- **Stayover Cleaning Log:** In HotSOS, the Room Attendant records linen change status per room per day.
- **Guest Card linen notes in Mews:** Captures any guest-requested change from default.
- **Pool Towel Log:** Paper log at the pool attendant stand, scanned weekly and filed.
- **Beach Towel Register:** Serial-number tracking maintained by the Director of Housekeeping.
- **Monthly Par Audit:** Completed by the Director of Housekeeping on the first Monday of each month.

## Escalation Triggers

Notify the Director of Housekeeping if:
- A room's linens show unusual damage beyond normal wear
- A guest specifically requests a brand, thread count, or linen type the property does not carry
- Any linen-related complaint is received at check-out
- Pool or beach towel loss spikes above the monthly target
