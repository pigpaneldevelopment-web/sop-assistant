# Restaurant Opening Procedure

**Property:** The Ironwood, Sedona, AZ
**Department:** Food & Beverage
**Document Owner:** F&B Supervisor
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

This procedure governs the opening of the restaurant and lobby bar at The Ironwood. A consistent opening sequence ensures the space is guest-ready, the team is aligned, and the POS is functional before the first guest is seated. Because our breakfast service begins at 7:00 AM with in-room dining already in flight, the opening team works on compressed time — the sequence below is built to that constraint.

## Scope

Applies to the opening F&B Supervisor, Servers, Bartenders, and any Expeditor on the opening shift. Covers the restaurant, lobby bar, and outdoor patio. In-room dining opening is governed by a separate SOP but is coordinated through this one.

## Responsibilities

- **F&B Supervisor:** Owns the opening checklist; verifies all steps complete before service starts; resolves any issue that would delay open.
- **Opening Server:** Completes the dining room and service station setup per the standards below.
- **Opening Bartender:** Completes the bar setup, verifies liquor par, and reports any shortage to the F&B Supervisor.
- **General Manager:** Reviews opening checklist sign-off from the prior day as part of morning operations.

## Timing

Opening tasks begin at 5:45 AM for a 7:00 AM breakfast service. The full sequence below is timed to 75 minutes with the opening team of three. Delays to any step below 60 minutes from open are escalated to the F&B Supervisor.

## Pre-Open Sequence

### 5:45 AM — Arrival and Walkthrough

1. Opening team clocks in through MICROS on the service station terminal.
2. F&B Supervisor performs a walkthrough of the restaurant, bar, and patio. Note any maintenance, cleaning, or setup issues.
3. F&B Supervisor logs the walkthrough in the Opening Checklist (digital form in the shared F&B drive).
4. Assign any maintenance request to Engineering via email, copying the General Manager for anything guest-visible.

### 5:55 AM — POS and Cash

1. F&B Supervisor powers on all MICROS terminals. Verify connection to the kitchen printer.
2. Run a test ticket through each terminal to confirm print.
3. Count the opening till for each server station and the bar. Standard opening float: $200 per station, $400 for the bar.
4. Confirm the credit card readers are online by running a $0.01 authorization. Void immediately.
5. Log any variance in the Cash Log before service opens — not after.

### 6:00 AM — Dining Room Setup

1. Turn on dining room lighting to "Breakfast" scene via the lighting panel.
2. Set HVAC to 68°F. On days forecast above 95°F, set to 66°F to buffer the patio door traffic.
3. Set table tops per standard: cloth napkin folded to right of charger, coffee cup and saucer upper right, water glass upper left, fresh flower in vase centered.
4. Fill water pitchers. Do not pre-pour water at tables until a guest is seated.
5. Confirm all tables have clean condiment caddies — sugar, sweetener, salt, pepper, hot sauce.
6. Stock the service station: coffee cups, saucers, spoons, straws, napkins, ramekins, bread plates.

### 6:15 AM — Bar and Coffee Station

1. Bartender unlocks the liquor cabinet and verifies bottle count against the closing par sheet from the prior night.
2. Report any bottle short of par to the F&B Supervisor before the 6:45 AM pre-shift.
3. Brew first pot of house blend coffee. Standard brew time: 6 minutes. Coffee is pulled from the carafe after 45 minutes and replaced with a fresh pot.
4. Start the decaf pot 10 minutes after the house blend.
5. Stock the espresso machine. Prime, purge the steam wand, and run a sacrificial shot to verify grind and temp.
6. Confirm milk, half-and-half, oat milk, and almond milk are stocked. Check expiration dates on each.
7. Slice lemons, limes, and any other service fruit. Place in labeled containers on the service station.

### 6:30 AM — Kitchen Coordination

1. F&B Supervisor checks in with the opening line cook. Confirm menu items available, any 86'd items, any special prep for a VIP or large table.
2. Review the breakfast reservation list from OPERA Cloud. Flag any group blocks, VIPs, or guests with dietary notes.
3. Relay dietary notes to the kitchen. Any allergy flag is handled per the Allergy and Dietary Restriction Handling SOP.

### 6:45 AM — Pre-Shift Briefing

The F&B Supervisor leads a brief standup with the opening team:

1. Review menu specials, 86'd items, and any wine or beverage features.
2. Review the reservation list: group blocks, VIPs, allergies, special occasions.
3. Assign sections. Confirm break schedule.
4. Remind the team of one service focus point for the shift — a specific standard to sharpen. Example: "Full table sweep every 10 minutes this morning — we got hit on that in yesterday's TrustYou feedback."

### 6:55 AM — Final Walkthrough

The F&B Supervisor performs a final walkthrough and signs the Opening Checklist as complete. Any item not complete at this point is escalated to the General Manager by cell phone.

## At Open (7:00 AM)

1. Unlock the dining room doors.
2. Position the host stand with menus, a reservation list, and the walk-in waitlist.
3. Confirm MICROS is running. Begin seating guests as they arrive.

## Common Failure Modes

- Opening float not counted before service — any later variance becomes impossible to attribute.
- Bar par not checked — a missing bottle during a 9:00 PM service creates a service gap that could have been solved at 6:00 AM with a call to the GM.
- Pre-shift briefing skipped because "we're behind" — the briefing is what keeps the team from being behind at 8:00 AM.

## Escalation Triggers

F&B Supervisor notifies the General Manager immediately if:
- Any MICROS terminal or card reader is down
- Opening float is off by more than $5
- A critical menu item is 86'd before service
- A team member does not show up and coverage cannot be secured by 6:30 AM
