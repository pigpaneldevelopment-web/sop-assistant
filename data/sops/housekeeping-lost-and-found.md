# Lost and Found Handling

**Property:** Salinas Point Inn, Central California Coast
**Department:** Housekeeping
**Document Owner:** Director of Housekeeping
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Lost and found handling protects both the guest and the property. A clean, auditable chain of custody — from the moment an item is found to the moment it is returned or discarded — prevents accusations of theft, resolves disputes quickly, and demonstrates operational integrity. This procedure defines how every item is logged, stored, returned, and disposed of at Salinas Point Inn.

## Scope

Applies to all Room Attendants, Inspectors, Front Desk Agents, and F&B staff who encounter guest items left behind in guest rooms, public spaces, the restaurant, or meeting spaces. The Director of Housekeeping owns the Lost and Found inventory.

## Responsibilities

- **Room Attendant:** Logs any found item in Mews during the clean; does not move the item until logged; bags and delivers to the Lost and Found room at shift end.
- **Inspector:** Verifies items found during inspection are logged; counter-signs high-value items.
- **Front Desk Agent:** Receives guest inquiries about lost items; searches the Lost and Found log; coordinates return.
- **Director of Housekeeping:** Maintains the Lost and Found inventory; authorizes disposal at the 90-day mark; handles any dispute.
- **General Manager:** Reviews any item valued over $500; approves any deviation from return or disposal policy.

## Item Categories

Items are handled by category because each carries different handling rules.

### Category 1 — Standard Items
Clothing, toiletries, books, chargers, sunglasses, water bottles, hats, general personal items.

### Category 2 — High-Value Items
Jewelry, watches, electronics (phones, tablets, laptops, cameras), wallets, passports, identification, cash, gift cards.

### Category 3 — Perishable Items
Food, beverages, flowers. These are discarded at the end of the shift they are found and logged as "discarded — perishable."

### Category 4 — Medication
All medication, prescription or over-the-counter. Handled per the Medication Found subsection below.

### Category 5 — Unattended/Suspicious Items
Items found in unusual places, items without clear ownership, anything that could be an intentional drop. Escalated immediately to the Manager on Duty.

## Standard Logging Procedure

Every found item is logged at the moment of discovery. No exceptions.

1. At the moment an item is found, the Room Attendant opens the Lost and Found section of Mews on their tablet.
2. Log the following fields: date found, time found, room number or location, finder's name, item description, category, condition.
3. Take a photograph of the item and attach it to the log entry.
4. For Category 1 items, place in a clear bag labeled with the log entry ID. Deliver to the Lost and Found room at shift end.
5. For Category 2 items, notify the Inspector immediately. Both sign off on the log entry before the item leaves the room. Deliver to the Front Desk for immediate lockbox storage — do not leave until the Front Desk Agent counter-signs receipt.
6. For Category 3 items, photograph, log as "discarded — perishable," and discard.
7. For Category 4 items, follow the Medication Found subsection below.
8. For Category 5 items, do not touch further. Exit the room, secure the door, and contact the Manager on Duty immediately.

## Medication Found

Medication carries elevated risk — both the guest's health (they may return looking for it) and the property's (incorrect handling could create liability).

1. Do not discard medication of any kind, including what appears to be a partial or empty bottle.
2. Photograph and log per standard procedure.
3. Bag separately from other items in a labeled medication bag.
4. Deliver to the Front Desk for lockbox storage.
5. If the bottle has a pharmacy label with guest name and phone, the Front Desk Agent contacts the guest within 2 hours using the number in Mews Guest Card or the pharmacy label, whichever has a working contact.
6. Retain medication for 30 days, then disposal per the Disposal subsection below.

## Guest Inquiries

When a guest contacts the property looking for a lost item:

1. Front Desk Agent opens the Lost and Found section of Mews.
2. Search by the dates the guest was in-house, then by description.
3. If a match is found, confirm details with the guest — color, brand, distinguishing feature — before confirming the property has the item.
4. For Category 1 items, offer mail-back at the guest's expense or pickup at the property.
5. For Category 2 items, offer mail-back via a trackable carrier (UPS, FedEx) at the guest's expense. Require a signed release emailed before shipment. For items valued over $500, the General Manager must approve shipment.
6. If no match is found, offer to add the inquiry to the search log. Inform the guest the team will follow up within 48 hours if the item surfaces.

## Return Procedure

When a guest returns to collect a lost item in person:

1. Verify the guest's identity with photo ID.
2. Retrieve the item from Lost and Found storage with the Inspector or a second staff member present for Category 2 items.
3. Guest confirms the item is theirs and signs the Lost and Found log as received, noting date and time.
4. Front Desk Agent updates the log entry to "Returned" with guest name and signature.

## Storage and Disposal

- **Category 1:** Held for 90 days in the Lost and Found room in labeled bags, organized by date found.
- **Category 2:** Held in the Front Desk lockbox for 90 days.
- **Category 3:** Discarded same shift.
- **Category 4:** Held for 30 days in locked medication storage, then surrendered to a pharmacy drop-box or local law enforcement medication disposal program. Do not flush or trash.
- **Category 5:** Disposition directed by the Manager on Duty or General Manager.

At the 90-day mark, the Director of Housekeeping reviews unclaimed Category 1 and 2 items. Category 1 items are donated to a local shelter or disposed of. Category 2 items valued over $500 are held an additional 30 days before the General Manager authorizes donation or disposal.

## Documentation

- Every found item generates a Mews Lost and Found log entry with photo.
- Disposal and donation events are logged with date, staff member, and disposition.
- The Director of Housekeeping performs a monthly audit of the Lost and Found inventory against the log.
