# Post-Departure Group Reconciliation

**Property:** Juniper Creek Ranch, Fredericksburg, TX
**Department:** Group Management
**Document Owner:** General Manager
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Post-departure reconciliation is where the group business actually closes. A successful group stay can still become a bad experience for the client — and a lost return booking — if the final invoice arrives late, contains errors, or doesn't reflect the agreements made during the stay. This procedure defines the reconciliation process at Juniper Creek Ranch from the morning after departure through the invoice send and, where applicable, through the final payment or dispute resolution.

## Scope

Applies to the General Manager, Front Desk Agents handling any outstanding folio items, and Accounting who processes the final invoice. Covers the period from group departure through final payment or close-out of the group engagement. Pre-arrival and in-stay billing procedures are covered by separate SOPs.

## Responsibilities

- **General Manager:** Owns the reconciliation; reviews and approves the final master folio; resolves any dispute with the client; signs off on the final invoice.
- **Front Desk Agent:** Flags any post-departure discovery (missed posting, unreturned key, damage report) to the GM within 24 hours.
- **Accounting:** Generates the final invoice; processes payment or direct-bill collection; reconciles revenue posting in Maestro.
- **Ownership:** Informed of any dispute or write-off exceeding $2,000.

## Reconciliation Timeline

Post-departure work follows a defined cadence. Staying on schedule is the single biggest driver of successful reconciliation.

| Milestone | Timing | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Post-departure audit | Morning after departure | GM |
| Final master folio draft | Within 24 hours of departure | GM |
| Client review copy sent | Within 48 hours of departure | GM |
| Client review window | 3–5 business days | Client |
| Final invoice sent | Within 7 business days of departure | Accounting |
| Payment received (credit card or ACH) | At invoice close for non-direct-bill | Accounting |
| Payment received (direct bill) | Net 30 from invoice | Client |
| Engagement closed | Upon payment clearance | Accounting |

## Step 1 — Post-Departure Audit

The morning after the group's departure, the GM performs a complete audit of the group's folios.

### Room-Level Audit

1. Pull the Group Master in Maestro.
2. Run the Group Rooms Report showing every reservation in the block with final folio status.
3. Verify every reservation shows as "Checked Out."
4. Any reservation still showing "Occupied" or "No-Show" is investigated:
   - "Occupied" means the FDA missed the check-out. Process retroactively.
   - "No-Show" means the guest never arrived. Confirm the no-show charge was processed per the No-Show Processing SOP.
5. Spot-check five reservations' folios for correct charge routing (guest-pay vs. master-pay).

### Master Folio Audit

1. Pull the Master Folio in Maestro.
2. Verify every contracted charge has posted:
   - Contracted meals (confirmed against BEOs)
   - Meeting room or event space rental
   - AV charges
   - Welcome amenity
   - Bus or transportation charges
   - Any other item specified in the contract
3. Verify no charges have mistakenly posted to master that should have gone to guest folios.
4. Cross-check the master folio total against the contract's projected revenue. Material variance (more than 5%) requires investigation.

### Common Mis-Routings to Watch For

- Gratuity on a contracted meal posted to guest rather than master (most common error).
- Audiovisual equipment posted as a separate line when the contract bundled it with meeting space.
- A spa charge routed to master when the contract limits master to F&B only.
- Damages or lost key charges not yet posted — ensure all incidentals have hit the folio.

## Step 2 — Missed Postings

Any posting missed during the stay is applied now.

1. Late F&B charges (e.g., a banquet that closed to MICROS after the audit window): post manually via the Banquet Event Charge function in Maestro, coded to the Group Master.
2. Damages or missing items discovered during departure clean: post as Damage / Loss Charge per the Guest Damage SOP, coded to the appropriate folio.
3. Lost key or amenity: post per standard charges.
4. Any service recovery credit promised to a guest but not yet applied: post now, note the reason.

## Step 3 — Final Master Folio Draft

Within 24 hours of departure, the GM produces a final master folio draft for client review.

1. Export the Master Folio from Maestro as PDF.
2. Review the PDF for:
   - All contracted line items present
   - Line items described in plain language (not internal codes)
   - Dates correct
   - Tax calculation correct
   - Any credit or adjustment line clearly labeled
3. Prepare a cover email referencing the group's contract and the stay, summarizing the high-level total.
4. Attach the final master folio PDF.
5. Call the client first — do not send the email as first contact. A 5-minute call establishes the tone: "We had a great group here, here's where we landed, I'm sending you the final recap to review."

## Step 4 — Client Review

The client reviews the draft for 3–5 business days.

1. Track the send date and expected review completion date.
2. If the client has not acknowledged receipt within 2 business days, follow up with a polite email. "Just making sure you received the final review from Saturday's group departure."
3. If the client comes back with questions, respond within 24 hours.
4. Documented client sign-off is required before the final invoice is issued. "Looks good — proceed with invoicing" by email from the billing contact is sufficient.

## Step 5 — Dispute Handling

If the client disputes a line item:

1. Do not become defensive. Thank them for the review and commit to looking into it.
2. Pull the backup: the BEO for a disputed F&B event, the signed contract page for a disputed inclusion, the POS ticket for a disputed charge.
3. Compare the dispute against the backup. The dispute is either:
   - Correct (our mistake): apologize, adjust, resend the revised master folio for review.
   - Partially correct: acknowledge the valid portion, explain the portion that stands, offer a compromise if warranted.
   - Not supported by the contract: politely explain, share the backup, hold the position.
4. Any adjustment over $500 requires GM sign-off (self-sign as GM is fine; the record matters for audit trail).
5. Any adjustment over $2,000 requires ownership notification before it is applied.
6. Document every decision and communication in the group's file.

## Step 6 — Final Invoice

Once the client has signed off on the draft, Accounting generates the final invoice.

1. Accounting opens the Group Master in Maestro.
2. Close the master folio. This locks the folio against further postings.
3. Generate the final invoice PDF.
4. For credit card settlement: process the card on file for the full balance; email the paid invoice as a receipt.
5. For ACH: send the invoice with wiring instructions; confirm payment receipt within 3 business days.
6. For direct bill: send the invoice with Net 30 terms; flag the group in the Accounts Receivable aging report.

## Step 7 — Direct Bill Collection

For direct-bill groups, Accounting monitors the AR aging.

1. Day 20 after invoice: friendly reminder email to the billing contact.
2. Day 30 after invoice: formal past-due notice if payment not received.
3. Day 45 after invoice: GM-signed escalation letter.
4. Day 60 after invoice: engage collections per property policy; notify ownership.

## Step 8 — Close-Out and Learnings

Once payment has cleared, the GM closes the group engagement.

1. Complete the Group Close-Out form in the shared drive:
   - Final contracted revenue vs. actual revenue
   - Attrition outcome (if applicable)
   - Any service incidents during the stay
   - Client satisfaction (based on post-stay conversation and/or review posts)
   - Return booking probability (GM's subjective assessment)
   - Key learnings for future groups
2. Archive the group folder:
   - Contract
   - Rooming list (original and as-processed)
   - Group Recap
   - BEOs
   - Master folio (final)
   - Client communications
   - Final invoice
3. Update the Group Revenue Report with the final numbers.
4. Note in the client's contact record (in the sales CRM) any notes for next year.

## Step 9 — Return Business Cultivation

For groups with strong return potential, the GM follows up proactively.

1. Within 30 days of departure: a thank-you note or gift from the GM to the primary client contact.
2. Within 60 days: a "save the date" suggestion for next year's dates if applicable.
3. Within 90 days: a check-in email asking about their event planning timeline for the following year.

## Common Failure Modes

- Sending the final invoice without a client review call — creates a "hey, what's this?" response and an avoidable back-and-forth.
- Posting late charges to master without client acknowledgment — makes the final total higher than what the client signed off on.
- Holding the final invoice for internal review until week 2 or 3 — the 7-business-day target exists because client memory of the stay is freshest in week 1.
- Escalating a dispute to ownership before exhausting reasonable resolution — signals a lack of ownership at the GM level.

## Documentation

- **Group Close-Out form** — standardized form in shared drive.
- **Final Master Folio PDF** — archived in group folder.
- **Client review email and response** — filed in group folder.
- **Final Invoice** — filed with Accounting.
- **AR Aging tracking** — for direct bill groups.

## Escalation Triggers

Contact ownership if:
- Dispute exceeds $2,000
- Direct bill has aged past 60 days without payment
- Any threat of legal action by the client
- Client has demanded a write-off that the GM is not authorized to grant
